The Fundamentals

Combat & the Stagger Loop

XVI is a character-action game wearing a Final Fantasy crest. Damage matters, but stagger matters more — almost everything you do is in service of breaking an enemy's Will and cashing in the window. Learn this loop and the rest of the game opens up.

01 · The Buttons

Your basic kit

Before any Eikon powers, Clive has a compact action toolkit. Everything below is always available and costs no resources.

02 · The Core Loop

Will, stagger & the damage window

Most enemies above trash level have two bars: HP (yellow) and a Will gauge (the segment below it). You almost never beat a big enemy by grinding HP — you break Will.

  • Drain the Will gauge. Hits chip it down. When it empties, the enemy is Staggered: knocked into a long helpless state with a damage multiplier that climbs toward 1.5× or more as you wail on them.
  • Partial Stagger. Drop the Will gauge to half and a "Stagger" prompt flickers — some abilities do bonus Will damage to a half-broken target, snowballing the break.
  • Two jobs, two toolsets. Some abilities are Will-breakers (they barely scratch HP but shred the gauge); others are damage-dumps (huge HP numbers, poor Will). You break with one set and dump with the other.
  • Don't waste the window. The instant an enemy staggers, unload your longest, highest-damage abilities — Gigaflare, Zantetsuken, the charged finishers — plus your Limit Break if it's ready. A clean stagger dump can erase a boss's whole health bar.
The mental model: build the gauge to zero as fast as you can with cheap Will abilities, then in the stagger window spend everything that hits hard. Holding a big damage cooldown for the stagger is the single biggest skill jump in the game.
03 · Defense

Precision Dodge, Counter & Titanic Block

There's no shield button in the base kit — your defense is timing. Dodging is also your best offense, because a perfect dodge buys a free punish.

  • Precision Dodge (R1, last-instant). Evade just before a blow connects and time briefly slows. You're invulnerable through it and perfectly placed to retaliate.
  • Precision Counter. Attack out of a Precision Dodge for Burning Strike — a boosted counter that does strong Will damage. Dodge-into-attack is the rhythm of every hard fight.
  • Titanic Block (Titan's Feat). If you equip Titan, his Feat is a parry: block with the right timing to negate a hit and open a heavy counter. A precise guard answers attacks a dodge can't.
  • Cinematic clashes. Big set-piece blows trigger a prompt — hit it to win a clash. These are spectacle, not failure states; missing one just costs damage.
04 · Your Wolf

Torgal, healing & the Limit Break

  • Torgal commands (D-pad). Send the wolf to Sic (attack/launch), Ravage (a damage flurry) or Heal (restores a chunk of Clive's HP, on a cooldown). Sic launches small enemies for air combos and keeps Will damage flowing while your abilities recharge. Folding Torgal into combos is most of the game's combo depth.
  • Potions, not a heal spell. You heal with Potions and High Potions (limited stock, restocked at the Hideaway). Torgal's Heal and certain abilities supplement them. Manage the count — running dry in a long Eikon fight is the usual death.
  • Limit Break (L3 + R3). A meter fills as you fight; pop it to enter a temporary state with greatly boosted damage and steady self-healing, and to break through some scripted moments. Save it to layer on top of a stagger for a burst that deletes health bars.
Magic Burst, againThe fastest way to raise your damage today: end every melee string with a tapped Magic Burst, and weave Torgal's Sic between ability cooldowns. Two free habits, no AP spent, and your Will damage roughly doubles.
05 · Accessibility

Timely rings & difficulty

XVI lets you tune the challenge with equippable accessories rather than a menu slider. Story Focused mode simply starts you with these "Timely" rings equipped; you can add or remove them in either mode at any time.

Timely accessoryEffect
Ring of Timely EvasionAuto-dodges most incoming attacks for you.
Ring of Timely StrikesPerforms elaborate combos from a single attack button.
Ring of Timely FocusSlows time when an enemy is about to strike, so you can react.
Ring of Timely HealingAuto-uses potions when your HP drops low.
Ring of Timely AssistanceHas Torgal act automatically.

Each Timely ring occupies an accessory slot, so playing on full assists trades away build slots — a natural nudge to drop them as you improve. Final Fantasy Mode, unlocked after a first clear, is the real difficulty step: remixed, tougher enemy placements and a higher level cap (see Progression).
