The Long Game

Progression, Gear & Endgame

XVI keeps its systems lean: light gear, a single smithy, and an endgame built on replay rather than grinding. Here's the Hideaway loop, which sidequests actually pay off, and everything that opens after the credits — Final Fantasy Mode, the trials, and the two DLC packs.

01 · Home base

The Hideaway

Your hub between chapters. Everything that grows your power lives here.

FacilityWhat it's for
BlacksmithCraft and reinforce weapons, armour and accessories from materials. The only place gear improves.
Merchant (Charon)Buy potions, materials and consumables; sell junk. Restock potions before every story push.
Hunt BoardAccept Notorious Mark hunts (see Bosses & Hunts) — your rare-material pipeline.
Arete StoneTraining mode (practice combos free of stakes & earn AP), replay past Eikon battles, and fast-travel (the Stagecoach).
The Patron's hallWhere sidequests are picked up and the Hideaway grows as you complete them.
Harpocrates & the TomesLore keeper — the "Thousand Tomes" and Active Time Lore. Lore quizzes can hand out useful items.
OrchestrionPlay unlocked music tracks — pure flavour.
02 · Gear

Smithing & the weapon chase

  • Weapons matter most. Each weapon has an Attack and a Stagger value. Reinforce your current weapon, or craft a new one from a recipe — recipes come from sidequests and hunts, materials from hunts and bosses.
  • Armour is simple. A few slots (belt / vambraces / body) for Defense and HP. There's little build depth here — equip the highest you can craft and move on.
  • Accessories are the real gear build — ability augments and combat boosts (covered on Builds). Craft and upgrade these as your hunt materials pile up.
  • The ultimate weapons are gated. The strongest base-game blade is locked behind Final Fantasy Mode materials; the overall ultimate, Gotterdammerung, requires The Rising Tide DLC and its toughest content. Don't expect to finish the weapon chain on a first, normal playthrough.
Don't over-grind gear. On a first run, just keep your weapon reinforced and craft armour when materials allow — the difficulty is tuned around abilities and skill, not gear score. Save the weapon chase for Final Fantasy Mode.
03 · Sidequests

Which ones to actually do

Sidequests range from flavour errands to genuinely build-relevant rewards. Watch the marker:

  • Star / crystal-marked quests are the ones that pay off — they raise your potion capacity, upgrade Torgal's kit, improve the Limit Break, and unlock gear recipes and ability augments.
  • Do the marked quests before each point of no return. A cluster of important sidequests opens just before the finale — clearing them first nets the best recipes, more potions, and Torgal's full ability set for the final stretch.
  • Pure-flavour errands still build the world and the Hideaway, but skip them on a tight schedule — you can mop up via replay afterward.
Few true missablesThanks to the world reopening and the stage-replay system, XVI has almost nothing you can permanently lose. The "completion" effort is breadth — hunts, quests, trials — not a fragile missable checklist.
04 · After the credits

Final Fantasy Mode & the trials

ModeWhat it is
Final Fantasy ModeNew Game+ on hard. Remixed, tougher enemy placements, a raised level cap, new and stronger gear/materials, and a stiffer stagger economy. The intended "real" playthrough for veterans — and where the top weapons become craftable.
Arcade ModeReplay any stage for a score and letter rank. Great for drilling combos and chasing S-ranks.
UltimaniacThe hardest Arcade tier — score-attack for the truly confident.
Chronolith TrialsTimed combat challenges with a restricted ability set; clearing them rewards unique accessories. A pure test of execution.

Platinum/100% effectively means clearing Final Fantasy Mode, finishing every hunt, S-ranking Arcade stages and beating the Chronoliths — a breadth-and-skill grind rather than a luck grind.

05 · Add-ons

The DLC

DLCAdds
Echoes of the FallenA new story chapter in the Sagespire tower, fresh gear and ability augments, and a brutal optional superboss. Best played late in the campaign or post-game.
The Rising TideThe Mysidia region, the Leviathan Eikon and its abilities, new sidequests, the Kairos Gate endless horde challenge, and the ultimate weapon Gotterdammerung.
Both DLCs unlock from save points / the world map once installed and you've reached the required story point. The Rising Tide in particular is endgame-grade — tackle it with a finished build, ideally on Final Fantasy Mode for the full weapon payoff.
