Final Fantasy VII Remake

Intergrade

The first chapter of the remake trilogy — the escape from Midgar, retold in full. Real-time action fused with the ATB bar, the pressure-and-stagger loop, Materia, and a per-weapon upgrade system. This is the foundation that FF7 Rebirth builds on.

Start Here

Core systems at a glance

  • Action + ATB. You move and attack in real time to fill ATB segments, then spend them on abilities, spells and items. Tactical Mode slows time to a crawl while you pick a command — the heart of good play.
  • Pressure → Stagger. Exploit weaknesses and the right tactics to Pressure an enemy, which fills the stagger gauge faster; a full gauge Staggers them for a big damage window. Everything funnels into that window.
  • Materia is the build system. Mako orbs slotted into weapons/armor, with linked slots for Support combos (Elemental, Magnify, Magic Efficiency). Five types: Magic, Support, Command, Complete, Summon.
  • Per-weapon upgrades, not a Folio. Each character has several weapons; each carries a Weapon Ability you learn permanently (via Proficiency) and an SP-based upgrade tree. This is different from Rebirth's shared Folio.
  • Hard Mode is the real endgame — no items, no MP regen on benches. It gates the best manuscripts and the Pride and Joy superboss (for Gotterdammerung). Intergrade adds the Yuffie INTERmission episode.
