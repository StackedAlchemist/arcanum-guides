Stellaris

A grand-strategy 4X where you grow a spacefaring empire from one system: explore, expand, exploit, exterminate. Your identity comes from Ethics, Authority and Civics; your power from economy, tech, traditions and fleet design. This guide leans on durable fundamentals plus the new nomadic layer, since the per-patch "best build" meta shifts constantly.

01 · The Loop

The core loop — the four X's

Stellaris runs on four pillars: eXplore, eXpand, eXploit, eXterminate.

  • Explore with Science Ships. Survey systems for deposits, anomalies, and archaeology sites. Early exploration sets up everything.
  • Expand by building Outposts (Starbases) to claim systems, then colonizing habitable worlds inside your borders. You can only colonize within your own territory.
  • Exploit by working planets: pops fill jobs, jobs produce resources (energy, minerals, food, alloys, research, unity, consumer goods, strategic resources).
  • Exterminate — or out-tech, out-grow, out-diplomacy. Victory can be military, but tall economic/tech empires win too.

Early game is exploration and expansion; mid game is consolidation and tech; late game is megastructures and surviving the end-game Crisis.

02 · Identity

Empire creation

Your empire is defined by three things at creation — the single most important set of choices in the game.

Ethics (3 points, fanatic = 2)

WarMilitarist (war bonuses, fire rate)Pacifist (stability, growth, tall play)

DiplomacyXenophile (trade, diplo, migration)Xenophobe (pop growth, closed borders)

Plus the gestalt ethics that replace the whole spread: Hive Mind and Machine Intelligence (no factions, no normal happiness, pops are drones).

Authority

How you're governed: Democratic (elections, egalitarian synergy), Oligarchic (flexible, long terms), Dictatorial/Imperial (autocratic, authoritarian synergy), the gestalt authorities, and Corporate (Megacorp: branch offices, trade focus).

Civics (2 at start, changeable later)

The mechanical edge: Technocracy (research engine), Byzantine Bureaucracy (unity/admin), Merchant Guilds (trade), Fanatic Purifiers (genocidal, no diplomacy), Rogue Servitors / Determined Exterminators (machine variants), Inward Perfection (pacifist tall powerhouse). Civics must be consistent with your ethics and authority.

The creation principle: ethics + authority + civics should reinforce one economic and military plan, not pull three directions. A Fanatic Materialist Technocracy is a tech empire; a Militarist Authoritarian Citizen-Service empire is a conquest machine. Decide what you're doing before you pick.
03 · The Engine

Economy and pops

The economy is a chain: pops work jobs → jobs make resources → resources build more capacity. Core resources: Energy Credits, Minerals, Food, Consumer Goods, Alloys, Research, Unity, Influence, and Strategic Resources (Motes/Gases/Crystals, plus Living Metal/Zro/Dark Matter).

  • Alloys win wars. Convert minerals to alloys via foundry jobs; a war economy is an alloy economy.
  • Influence is a bottleneck, not an afterthought. It gates outpost claims, edicts, and diplomacy. Don't overspend early.
  • Planet designations matter. Set a designation (mining, tech, forge world) to boost matching jobs. In 4.4, designation bonuses rose and planet automation improved, so a designated + automated planet lands close to expectation.
  • Pops fill jobs by fit. 4.3–4.4 reworked job competition: better-suited pops displace worse ones over time, higher strata turn over slowly, and unemployment was removed (pops drop to a fallback category). Hand-manage your capital; automate secondary worlds.
  • Empire size raises tech and tradition cost. Tall stays small and efficient; wide sprawls and pays the penalty but out-produces by volume. Both are viable.
04 · Knowledge

Research and the tech tree

Three categories run in parallel: Physics (energy, shields, research, psionics), Society (biology, statecraft, ascension), Engineering (materials, propulsion, voidcraft, armor/alloys).

Research is a semi-random card draw: you're offered a few techs per category and pick one; finishing reveals new draws. Scientist traits, Technocracy, and being on an ascension path weight the draws — in 4.4, key ascension-path techs get higher draw chances when you're on that path.

Priorities most games: early economy/growth tech, then your win-condition tech (weapons/ships for conquest, research/unity multipliers for tall, robots/genetics for pop output). Always keep all three categories running — idle research is wasted.
05 · Doctrine

Traditions & Ascension Perks

Traditions are unity-bought trees (Expansion, Discovery, Domination, Prosperity, Supremacy, Diplomacy…). Each completed tree unlocks an Ascension Perk slot. Slots and picks are deliberately scarce — that scarcity is by design — so choose trees that match your plan.

Ascension Perks are empire-wide bonuses and gates to ascension paths, megastructures, and ambitions. Notable: the megastructure unlocks, the three ascension-path perks, and in 4.4 the reworked Defender of the Galaxy ambition and the Wanderlust nomad perk.

06 · Transcendence

Ascension paths

The big mid-game identity commitment. Pick one lane (dabbling is possible, but going deep pulls heavy ethics attraction):

  • Psionic (Spiritualist-leaning): tap the Shroud for powerful, RNG-flavored bonuses and patrons. High ceiling, some risk.
  • Genetic (biology): heavily mod traits, clone/tailor pops; strong output and adaptability.
  • Cybernetic (Materialist/Spiritualist friendly): cyborg pops, solid all-round bonuses; bridges organic and machine.
  • Synthetic (Materialist): convert pops to synths for top-tier output — the classic robot ascension.
  • Virtuality / Nanotech and newer DLC lanes: Virtuality (a Machine route) is a strong tall/compact play and, per the devs, works well even for Nomads.

No single "best" path — match it to your empire. Synthetic and Genetic are the reliable output kings; Psionic has the flashiest highs.

07 · The Sword

Fleet design and war

Combat is fleet-vs-fleet in real time; you design the ships, then let them fight.

  • Counter the defense layers. Ships have Hull, Armor, Shields. Energy weapons shred armor and partly bypass shields; kinetics chew shields; missiles/strike craft bypass shield/PD dynamics. Mix weapon types — the classic baseline is roughly half kinetic, half energy.
  • Defense answers the threat. Shields vs energy-heavy enemies, armor vs kinetic, Point-Defense/flak vs missiles and strike craft.
  • Bigger hulls usually win once fielded: Corvette → Destroyer → Cruiser → Battleship → Titan, plus the Juggernaut (mobile shipyard) and Colossus (planet-killer). Mixed-weapon battleships are a hard baseline to beat.
  • Assess before you commit. A skull icon means the enemy fleet heavily outguns you — inspect the loadout and build a counter rather than feeding it ships.
  • Make armies before war. Space combat takes the system; ground armies take the planets. You must occupy systems and colonies to force surrender. Armies die fast to orbital fire — land only after the system is secured.
  • Go over fleet cap to win, then downsize. Exceeding command cap costs upkeep but can win a war; trim after.
New in 4.4Fleets fight further from system center, ships built at a Juggernaut/Arkship auto-follow it, and the AI upgrades its fleets more readily.
08 · The Senate

Diplomacy, Federations, the Galactic Community

  • Federations: alliances with shared fleets and levels; powerful, but the AI is an unreliable commander — don't bet survival on allies fighting well.
  • Galactic Community: a galaxy-wide senate; diplomatic weight decides votes. You can become Custodian and potentially form the Galactic Imperium. In 4.4, Defender of the Galaxy became a full five-level Ambition: cooperate, take Custodianship, build the Galactic Defense Force, run the Paladin Initiative, and win by defeating two end-game Crises then abdicating.
  • Subjects/vassals: overlordship with specialist subject types (note: nomadic empires can't use the Overlord specialist subject types).

In 4.4 you can also negotiate to join or leave an ongoing war as a non-primary, and fully-occupied empires now suffer escalating war-exhaustion/attrition so stomps end faster.

09 · Wonders

Megastructures

Late-game wonders that set an empire's ceiling (gated behind Ascension Perks and tech): Dyson Sphere (energy), Matter Decompressor (minerals), Science Nexus (research), Ring World (population), Sentry Array (galaxy intel), plus crisis-path structures.

New in 4.4The Stellar Cannon — a purely offensive megastructure on a neutron star/pulsar — fires your entire energy stockpile at an enemy system after a 140-day charge, dealing ship damage and colony devastation (10/25/50% by tier). Shots over 50,000 energy also shift planets one step up the dryness scale (long-range terraforming that trips Galactic Community resolutions). Nomads get Arkship Spinal Mounts that act like mobile kilostructures (Stellar Engine, Exodus Jump, etc.).
10 · The New Way

The Nomads layer (4.4)

Nomads adds an entirely different way to play: empires that never claim the map.

  • Arkships replace your homeworld — massive mobile capitals that are colony, shipyard and warship at once, in three classes (Military, Scientific, Civilian), each upgradeable through three tiers with modules, systems and late-game Spinal Mounts. An Arkship maxes at planet size 27, guaranteed, and can flee threats.
  • Waystations & Waylines: build Waystations to harvest a system without claiming it; adjacent Waystations link into Waylines that spread trade and modifiers. Two nomad empires can share networks via a Wayline Pact.
  • Contracts: settled empires issue task contracts (military, science, resource, diplomacy); nomads fulfill them for trade, opinion and trust. A settled+nomad co-op game can be genuinely symbiotic.
  • Operational Reserves: nomads merge energy and minerals into one fungible pool that constantly drains — it's intended to sit low (not a deficit emergency). The fix for a low reserve is always "go harvest more."
  • Settling & Embarking: after Adaptability or Versatility you can Settle onto an empty planet; settled empires can Embark onto Arkships (turning starbases into Waystations). The clean escape hatch for Doomsday-origin empires.
  • New origins: Voidfarers, Heirs of the Khan, The Sacred Path, Forever Cruise — plus new civics (Caravan Masters, Void Reavers, Star Seekers, Flight Schools, Deep Sleep) and six nomad-exclusive Paragons via the Champion's Forge Live! enclave.
Nomad warfare is different: Arkships can't be bombarded or invaded, only killed by ship combat (hull damage = devastation). Nomads can't claim planets (invade, occupy, then release or vassalize), take other nomads' Arkships via the Nomad Raid CB, and only need military superiority (not economy+research+military) to subjugate.
11 · The Test

Mid & late game: crises

  • Mid-game: the Great Khan (Marauders unite under a conqueror), wars, and awakening Fallen Empires.
  • End-game Crisis (timed by your end-game year): a galaxy-ender — the Prethoryn Scourge (devouring swarm), the Unbidden (extradimensional invaders), or the Contingency (rogue AI). A tech/alloy lead and a prepared fleet are how you survive.
  • Player crisis paths: you can become the crisis (Nemesis). 4.4's Defender of the Galaxy is the inverse — the "good guy" ambition built to fight crises.

Set the end-game year and crisis strength at galaxy creation to control pacing; 4.4 added more sliders (including Fallen/Awakened Empire strength).

12 · The First Hour

Opening playbook

A durable early sequence for a standard settled empire:

  1. Survey aggressively. Build a second Science Ship and hire a scientist immediately; explore in all directions.
  2. Grab choke points and good systems with early Outposts, but watch Influence — don't claim junk.
  3. Get your economy positive (energy and minerals first), then start an alloy base for ships.
  4. Colonize guaranteed habitables and nearby good worlds before settling into build-up.
  5. Pick a first tradition tree matching your plan (Expansion wide, Discovery tech, Prosperity economy).
  6. Build a small starting fleet and design your corvettes (turn off auto-best-design so you control loadouts).
  7. Plan your ascension lane by mid-game and steer research toward it.
In the ship designer at game start, uncheck "auto-generate designs" so you keep manual control of your loadouts.
13 · Field Notes

Durable tips

  • Keep all three research categories running at all times. Idle labs are wasted output.
  • Alloys are the war currency. Build foundry capacity before you plan a conquest.
  • Don't trust the AI to win your wars in a Federation; bring your own decisive fleet.
  • Designate + automate secondary planets; hand-manage your capital and key worlds only.
  • Mixed weapons, answered defenses. Never field a mono-weapon fleet a single counter shuts down.
  • Watch Influence like a resource, because it is one. It gates expansion and diplomacy.
  • For Nomads, stay in motion. Your strength is harvesting, Waylines and contracts, not turtling. You'll never out-defend a settled empire's system.
  • Set end-game year and crisis strength to taste — the single biggest pacing lever.
  • Back up saves before a major patch. Compatibility isn't guaranteed; 4.4 auto-disables mod lists on a major update (re-enable once mods update).
