The New Way

The Nomads Layer & Crises

The Nomads DLC adds an entirely different way to play — empires that never claim the map — and the back half of every game still ends in a galaxy-shaking test. Here's both.

10 · The New Way

The Nomads layer (4.4)

Nomads adds an entirely different way to play: empires that never claim the map.

  • Arkships replace your homeworld — massive mobile capitals that are colony, shipyard and warship at once, in three classes (Military, Scientific, Civilian), each upgradeable through three tiers with modules, systems and late-game Spinal Mounts. An Arkship maxes at planet size 27, guaranteed, and can flee threats.
  • Waystations & Waylines: build Waystations to harvest a system without claiming it; adjacent Waystations link into Waylines that spread trade and modifiers. Two nomad empires can share networks via a Wayline Pact.
  • Contracts: settled empires issue task contracts (military, science, resource, diplomacy); nomads fulfill them for trade, opinion and trust. A settled+nomad co-op game can be genuinely symbiotic.
  • Operational Reserves: nomads merge energy and minerals into one fungible pool that constantly drains — it's intended to sit low (not a deficit emergency). The fix for a low reserve is always "go harvest more."
  • Settling & Embarking: after Adaptability or Versatility you can Settle onto an empty planet; settled empires can Embark onto Arkships (turning starbases into Waystations). The clean escape hatch for Doomsday-origin empires.
  • New origins: Voidfarers, Heirs of the Khan, The Sacred Path, Forever Cruise — plus new civics (Caravan Masters, Void Reavers, Star Seekers, Flight Schools, Deep Sleep) and six nomad-exclusive Paragons via the Champion's Forge Live! enclave.
Nomad warfare is different: Arkships can't be bombarded or invaded, only killed by ship combat (hull damage = devastation). Nomads can't claim planets (invade, occupy, then release or vassalize), take other nomads' Arkships via the Nomad Raid CB, and only need military superiority (not economy+research+military) to subjugate.
11 · The Test

Mid & late game: crises

Every game escalates toward galaxy-shaking threats. The mid-game brings the Great Khan (Marauders unite under a conqueror), regional wars, and awakening Fallen Empires. The real test is the end-game Crisis, timed by the end-game year you set at galaxy creation.

Set the end-game year and crisis strength at galaxy creation to control pacing — the single biggest lever on difficulty. 4.4 added more sliders (including Fallen/Awakened Empire strength).

⚠ Spoiler — the end-game crises & how to beat them

One of three galaxy-enders spawns near the end-game year. Each is beaten the same way at heart: a decisive tech/alloy lead, fleet superiority, and chokepoint defense — but each has a weakness worth tailoring for.

CrisisWhat it isEdge against it
Prethoryn ScourgeAn extragalactic devouring swarm of bio-ships.Energy weapons & armor; their hull-heavy ships melt to focused fire. Psionic/Spiritualist tools help.
Unbidden (Extradimensionals)Energy beings pouring through a dimensional portal; shield-heavy.Kinetics to strip shields; rush and close the portal anchor to stop reinforcements.
The ContingencyAn ancient rogue-AI doomsday protocol that activates AI/synths.Destroy the Ghost Signal hub worlds; mixed weapons. Synthetic empires get a special warning.

You can also become the crisis. The Nemesis Aspiration lets you menace the galaxy and unleash a Star-Eater to win by annihilation. 4.4's Defender of the Galaxy is its inverse — a five-level "good guy" ambition built to fight crises, won by defeating two end-game Crises and then abdicating.
