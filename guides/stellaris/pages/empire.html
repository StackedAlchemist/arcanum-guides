The Foundation

Empire, Economy & Research

Everything downstream — your wars, your tech, your win condition — is set by three choices at creation and the resource engine you build on top of them. Get the foundation coherent and the rest follows.

02 · Identity

Empire creation

Your empire is defined by three things at creation — the single most important set of choices in the game.

Ethics (3 points, fanatic = 2)

AxisPole APole B
WarMilitarist (war bonuses, fire rate)Pacifist (stability, growth, tall play)
SocietyAuthoritarian (slavery, worker output)Egalitarian (specialist output, faction influence)
DiplomacyXenophile (trade, diplo, migration)Xenophobe (pop growth, closed borders)
KnowledgeMaterialist (research, robots)Spiritualist (unity, psionics, anti-AI)

Plus the gestalt ethics that replace the whole spread: Hive Mind and Machine Intelligence (no factions, no normal happiness, pops are drones).

Authority

How you're governed: Democratic (elections, egalitarian synergy), Oligarchic (flexible, long terms), Dictatorial/Imperial (autocratic, authoritarian synergy), the gestalt authorities, and Corporate (Megacorp: branch offices, trade focus).

Civics (2 at start, changeable later)

The mechanical edge: Technocracy (research engine), Byzantine Bureaucracy (unity/admin), Merchant Guilds (trade), Fanatic Purifiers (genocidal, no diplomacy), Rogue Servitors / Determined Exterminators (machine variants), Inward Perfection (pacifist tall powerhouse). Civics must be consistent with your ethics and authority.

The creation principle: ethics + authority + civics should reinforce one economic and military plan, not pull three directions. A Fanatic Materialist Technocracy is a tech empire; a Militarist Authoritarian Citizen-Service empire is a conquest machine. Decide what you're doing before you pick.
03 · The Engine

Economy and pops

The economy is a chain: pops work jobs → jobs make resources → resources build more capacity. Core resources: Energy Credits, Minerals, Food, Consumer Goods, Alloys, Research, Unity, Influence, and Strategic Resources (Motes/Gases/Crystals, plus Living Metal/Zro/Dark Matter).

  • Alloys win wars. Convert minerals to alloys via foundry jobs; a war economy is an alloy economy.
  • Influence is a bottleneck, not an afterthought. It gates outpost claims, edicts, and diplomacy. Don't overspend early.
  • Planet designations matter. Set a designation (mining, tech, forge world) to boost matching jobs. In 4.4, designation bonuses rose and planet automation improved.
  • Pops fill jobs by fit. 4.3–4.4 reworked job competition: better-suited pops displace worse ones over time, and unemployment was removed (pops drop to a fallback category). Hand-manage your capital; automate secondary worlds.
  • Empire size raises tech and tradition cost. Tall stays small and efficient; wide sprawls and pays the penalty but out-produces by volume. Both are viable.
04 · Knowledge

Research and the tech tree

Three categories run in parallel: Physics (energy, shields, research, psionics), Society (biology, statecraft, ascension), Engineering (materials, propulsion, voidcraft, armor/alloys).

Research is a semi-random card draw: you're offered a few techs per category and pick one; finishing reveals new draws. Scientist traits, Technocracy, and being on an ascension path weight the draws — in 4.4, key ascension-path techs get higher draw chances when you're on that path.

Priorities most games: early economy/growth tech, then your win-condition tech (weapons/ships for conquest, research/unity multipliers for tall, robots/genetics for pop output). Always keep all three categories running — idle research is wasted.
