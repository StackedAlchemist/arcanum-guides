12 · The First Hour

Opening playbook

A durable early sequence for a standard settled empire:

  1. Survey aggressively. Build a second Science Ship and hire a scientist immediately; explore in all directions.
  2. Grab choke points and good systems with early Outposts, but watch Influence — don't claim junk.
  3. Get your economy positive (energy and minerals first), then start an alloy base for ships.
  4. Colonize guaranteed habitables and nearby good worlds before settling into build-up.
  5. Pick a first tradition tree matching your plan (Expansion wide, Discovery tech, Prosperity economy).
  6. Build a small starting fleet and design your corvettes (turn off auto-best-design so you control loadouts).
  7. Plan your ascension lane by mid-game and steer research toward it.
In the ship designer at game start, uncheck "auto-generate designs" so you keep manual control of your loadouts.
13 · Field Notes

Durable tips

  • Keep all three research categories running at all times. Idle labs are wasted output.
  • Alloys are the war currency. Build foundry capacity before you plan a conquest.
  • Don't trust the AI to win your wars in a Federation; bring your own decisive fleet.
  • Designate + automate secondary planets; hand-manage your capital and key worlds only.
  • Mixed weapons, answered defenses. Never field a mono-weapon fleet a single counter shuts down.
  • Watch Influence like a resource, because it is one. It gates expansion and diplomacy.
  • For Nomads, stay in motion. Your strength is harvesting, Waylines and contracts, not turtling. You'll never out-defend a settled empire's system.
  • Set end-game year and crisis strength to taste — the single biggest pacing lever.
  • Back up saves before a major patch. Compatibility isn't guaranteed; 4.4 auto-disables mod lists on a major update (re-enable once mods update).
