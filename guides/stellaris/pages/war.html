Fleet, Diplomacy & Megastructures

How you actually impose your will — whether by a mixed-weapon battlefleet, a senate vote, or a wonder that sets your economic ceiling. The three levers of a great power.

07 · The Sword

Fleet design and war

Combat is fleet-vs-fleet in real time; you design the ships, then let them fight.

  • Counter the defense layers. Ships have Hull, Armor, Shields. Kinetics beat shields; energy beats armor (and is weak vs shields); missiles/torpedoes bypass shields. Mix weapon types — the classic baseline is roughly half kinetic, half energy.
  • Defense answers the threat. Shields vs energy-heavy enemies, armor vs kinetic, Point-Defense/flak vs missiles and strike craft.
  • Bigger hulls usually win once fielded: Corvette → Destroyer → Cruiser → Battleship → Titan, plus the Juggernaut (mobile shipyard) and Colossus (planet-killer). Mixed-weapon battleships are a hard baseline to beat.
  • Assess before you commit. A skull icon means the enemy fleet heavily outguns you — inspect the loadout and build a counter rather than feeding it ships.
  • Make armies before war. Space combat takes the system; ground armies take the planets. You must occupy systems and colonies to force surrender. Armies die fast to orbital fire — land only after the system is secured.
  • Go over fleet cap to win, then downsize. Exceeding command cap costs upkeep but can win a war; trim after.

08 · The Senate

Diplomacy, Federations, the Galactic Community

  • Federations: alliances with shared fleets and levels; powerful, but the AI is an unreliable commander — don't bet survival on allies fighting well.
  • Galactic Community: a galaxy-wide senate; diplomatic weight decides votes. You can become Custodian and potentially form the Galactic Imperium. In 4.4, Defender of the Galaxy became a full five-level Ambition: cooperate, take Custodianship, build the Galactic Defense Force, run the Paladin Initiative, and win by defeating two end-game Crises then abdicating.
  • Subjects/vassals: overlordship with specialist subject types (note: nomadic empires can't use the Overlord specialist subject types).

In 4.4 you can also negotiate to join or leave an ongoing war as a non-primary, and fully-occupied empires now suffer escalating war-exhaustion/attrition so stomps end faster.

New in 4.4The Stellar Cannon — a purely offensive megastructure on a neutron star/pulsar — fires your entire energy stockpile at an enemy system after a 140-day charge, dealing ship damage and colony devastation (10/25/50% by tier). Shots over 50,000 energy also shift planets one step up the dryness scale (long-range terraforming that trips Galactic Community resolutions). Nomads get Arkship Spinal Mounts that act like mobile kilostructures (Stellar Engine, Exodus Jump, etc.).
