Boss Guide

Universal Rule: Always use Assess on new bosses in their first phase. It reveals pressure conditions, weaknesses, and immunities. Stagger window is your highest damage period — save ATB Boost, Limit Breaks, and charged abilities for it.
Chapters 1 – 2 / Grasslands & Mythril Mines
01 Midgarsormr Grasslands — Chapter 1 ▾
Lightning Weak Wind Weak
Pressure Condition
Hit with Lightning or Wind materia. Melee while it's grounded also builds stagger.
Recommended Party
Cloud + Aerith + Barret (story-locked early)

The game's first real boss. Uses wide sweeping tail attacks and breath attacks. Stand to the side, not directly behind — tail sweep has massive range. Barret is invaluable here for ranged damage when Midgarsormr goes airborne. Cast Thunder to apply pressure, then attack the body once grounded.

Watch For: Gust attack — multi-hit wind damage. Move perpendicular to it. During aerial phase, only Barret and magic attacks can hit — switch to Barret and use Thunder.
02 Mythril Golem Mythril Mines — Chapter 2 ▾
Lightning Weak Stagger: Arms First
Pressure Condition
Destroy both arms separately to expose the core. Lightning causes pressure on arms.
Key Ability to Dodge
Fist Slam — telegraphed by raised fist. Roll away, not toward.

Two-phase fight. Phase 1: destroy both arms using focused attacks and Lightning. Once both arms are destroyed, the core is exposed and vulnerable. Phase 2: the Golem fights with its core directly exposed — stagger it for massive damage during this window.

Watch For: Stone Fling — throws arena debris for high damage. Can stagger your party. Keep HP above 50% before the boss enters Phase 2.
03 Failed Experiment Mythril Mines — Chapter 2 ▾
Ice Weak Fire Immune
Pressure Condition
Hit with Ice repeatedly. Sustained cold damage causes it to slow, then pressure.
Recommended Party
Cloud + Aerith (Blizzard spam) + Barret

A grotesque Shinra experiment with multiple limbs. Focus Ice attacks on the central body mass. Its tentacle attacks have long reach — keep some distance and dash in to attack, then pull back. Aerith casting Blizzara inside a Ward makes short work of this fight.

Watch For: Chemical Spray — inflicts Poison on the whole party. Have Antidotes ready or keep Aerith's Esuna available. Poisoned characters lose HP rapidly.
Chapters 3 – 4 / Junon Region
04 Gi Nattak Junon Outskirts — Chapter 3 ▾
Fire Weak Undead Mechanic
Pressure Condition
Hit with Fire. Also: Phoenix Downs deal massive damage due to undead typing.
Key Trick
Phoenix Down = instant death on all enemies in its undead form. Use items!

Gi Nattak is undead — this completely flips item usage. Heal items damage it, damage items heal it. Most importantly: Phoenix Downs deal enormous damage. Throw a Phoenix Down to skip its HP bar. Fire spells cause pressure. Standard healing items will restore its HP — use only the undead-exploit method.

Watch For: Soul Fire — spawns spirit enemies that harass your party. Kill them with Fire first or they overwhelm you. Don't ignore adds during this fight.
05 Jenova Emergent Junon — Chapter 4 ▾
Lightning Weak Water Weak Multiple Phases
Pressure Condition
Phase 1: Lightning. Phase 2: focus Arm appendages to expose core. Phase 3: sustained damage.
Recommended Party
Cloud + Aerith + Barret. Barret for airborne phase.
Phase 1 — Emergent Form

Jenova emerges from a tank of Mako. Use Thunder/Thundara to pressure. It will use Regen on itself — dispel it immediately with Aerith or the regen will outpace your damage.

Phase 2 — Aerial Form

Jenova becomes airborne. Barret is essential here — switch to him immediately. Attack the tentacle appendages to expose the main body. Once appendages are destroyed, it descends for a major stagger window.

Watch For: Abyssal Maelstrom — massive AoE dark damage. Use Aerith's Planet's Protection if you have it. Otherwise maintain high HP and have Cure ready. This attack is the primary party wipe source.
Chapters 5 – 6 / Corel & Gongaga
06 Grasptropod Corel Region — Chapter 5 ▾
Lightning Weak Wind Weak
Pressure Condition
Hit belly while it rears up on hind legs. Lightning causes instant pressure.
Stagger Target
Focus stagger attacks on the underbelly during pressure windows.

A large crustacean-type enemy. Wait for it to rear up on its hind legs — hit the exposed belly with Thunder for instant pressure. Keep range during its spinning claw attack. Yuffie is excellent here if she's in your party — Ninjutsu with Lightning element wrecks it.

Watch For: Spinning Claw — wide horizontal sweep that hits the entire front arc. Back away immediately when it begins spinning. If caught, it deals very high damage and likely stuns.
07 Abzu Gongaga Region — Chapter 6 ▾
Ice Weak Lightning Weak Multi-Phase
Pressure Condition
Phase 1: Ice or Lightning sustained. Phase 2: hit exposed head when rearing up.
Recommended Party
Cloud + Aerith + Yuffie (best elemental coverage)

Abzu is a serpentine boss with two distinct phases. Phase 1 it stays on the ground — hit with Ice and Lightning to pressure, attack the head. Phase 2 it rears up high — only its head is targetable and it gains new powerful attacks. Switch to Barret for sustained ranged hits when the head is elevated.

Watch For: Crush — grabs a character and deals massive damage. Cannot be dodged once telegraphed but can be interrupted with stagger. Also watch for Poison Spit — inflicts Poison on multiple party members simultaneously.
Chapters 7 – 8 / Cosmo Canyon & Nibel
08 Titan Cosmo Canyon — Chapter 7 ▾
Lightning Weak Wind Weak Summon Battle
Pressure Condition
Hit fists/arms with Lightning when Titan plants them in the ground to brace. Both arms pressured = stagger.
Recommended Party
Cloud + Aerith + Barret. Barret for reach on elevated attacks.

The first Summon battle of the game. Titan is enormous — most attacks target limbs. When he plants both fists into the earth, focus Lightning on both fists simultaneously to pressure both. This is your primary stagger route. His Landslide attack has very high range — dodge roll away from his body during windup.

Second Form

After losing ~40% HP, Titan shifts stance. His attacks become faster and he gains an air slam. The stagger mechanic is the same — hit both fists with Lightning when planted. Use Aerith's Ward to amplify Thundara casts for the biggest stagger buildup.

Watch For: Gaia's Wrath — his most powerful attack. High damage to all characters. Heal to full HP before this triggers (watch for the big wind-up animation). Planet's Protection from Aerith completely nullifies it if timed correctly.
09 Lost Number Mt. Nibel — Chapter 8 ▾
Ice Weak (Phase 1) Lightning Weak (Phase 2) Split Phase
Pressure Condition
Phase 1: Ice breaks its outer shell. Phase 2: Lightning to exposed core.
Key Note
Split into two smaller forms at ~50% HP. Focus one at a time.

Massive Shinra experiment. Covered in a tough outer layer weak to Ice — blast it with Blizzara until it cracks. Once cracked, the exposed core takes massive bonus damage from Lightning. At ~50% HP, it splits into two smaller versions — focus one down immediately before they combine their attacks.

Watch For: Heavy Strike — high damage physical that ignores some defenses. Stay at mid-range during melee phases. The split phase is the most dangerous — maintain distance and pick off one form with Lightning before engaging the second.
Chapters 11 – 14 / Ancient Forest & Endgame
10 Materia Guardian Ancient Forest — Chapter 11 ▾
Ice Weak Wind Weak High HP Pool
Pressure Condition
Hit elemental nodes on its body with the matching opposite element. Ice weakens the fire nodes, etc.
Recommended Party
Cloud + Aerith + Yuffie — maximum elemental flexibility

A guardian spirit with elemental nodes across its body. Each node has a specific weakness — Assess reveals them. Yuffie's ability to switch elements on the fly is uniquely powerful here. Destroy all active nodes to pressure the core, then stagger and burst. Aerith's Ward dramatically increases the speed at which you can rotate elements.

Watch For: Elemental Burst — massive AoE blast from the dominant active node. If you haven't destroyed the fire nodes and it charges fire burst, your whole party can go down. Prioritize destroying the dominant node first, even before attempting stagger.
11 Bahamut Arisen Combat Simulator / Chapter 11+ ▾
Wind Weak Countdown Mechanic
Pressure Condition
Deal enough damage during ground phase to pressure. Sustained wind attacks speed up stagger.
Megaflare Counter
Inflict Interrupt conditions to reset the countdown. Stagger BEFORE counter reaches 0.

Bahamut uses a Megaflare countdown — when it reaches zero, the attack will likely kill your party. You must stagger or interrupt him before the counter ends. Focus maximum damage from the start, don't pace yourself. Yuffie with Wind element Ninjutsu deals exceptional damage here.

Bahamut Arisen Phase

Second form is faster and the countdown speeds up. Planet's Protection should be saved for Megaflare if you can't stagger in time. ATB Boost + Limit Break combo in the stagger window should deal over half his HP. This fight rewards offensive aggression over cautious play.

Watch For: Megaflare — party wipe unless you have Planet's Protection active. Watch the counter at all times. If the counter hits 1 and you can't stagger: activate Planet's Protection immediately, take the hit, then respond.
12 Sephiroth — Final Encounter Chapter 14 — Endgame ▾
All Elements 4 Phases Best Gear Required
Pressure Condition
Each phase has a different pressure trigger — Assess each form. Generally: exploit the elemental opposite of whatever he's currently using.
Recommended Party
Cloud + Aerith + Tifa or Yuffie. Best gear. Full Limits stocked.
Phase 1 — Sephiroth (Human Form)

Sephiroth attacks fast with Masamune combos. Block in Punisher Mode — Cloud counters automatically. Synergy Skills trigger frequently in this phase. Bait his basic combo, counter, deal damage. His parrying AI is sophisticated — don't spam the same ability repeatedly.

Phase 2 — One-Winged Angel

He gains his iconic wing and power dramatically increases. Shadow Flare will target characters — move away from the target indicator immediately. Aerith should be kept alive at all costs — she's your only reliable healer for this phase.

Phase 3 — Shattered Form

The arena changes. His movement speed increases. This is the phase where most parties get wiped by running out of MP on Hard Mode — conserve every spell. Only cast inside Wards. Use physical attacks primarily.

Phase 4 — Supernova Warning

Final phase. Has a Supernova countdown like Bahamut's Megaflare. Max damage burst is your only option — there is no safe way through this phase. ATB Boost, all Limit Breaks, full Yuffie Ninjutsu combo. Kill him before the counter ends.

Watch For: Octaslash — 8-hit combo. Dodge back completely — don't try to block or counter. Also: Heartless Angel reduces all party members to 1 HP. Immediately use Cura (Magnify) before any follow-up attack lands.
