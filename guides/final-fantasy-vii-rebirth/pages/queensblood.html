Card Game Guide

Queen's Blood

Rules Overview

How Queen's Blood Works

Core Objective

Control more of the 3×3 grid than your opponent at the end of all rounds. Each lane (column) is won by whoever has the higher total power on their side. Win 2 of the 3 lanes to win the match.

Pips

Each card has 1, 2, or 3 pips — the number of squares it claims when played. 1-pip cards claim 1 square, 3-pip cards can claim large areas. High-pip cards are powerful but limited.

Power

Each card has a power value (the number in the corner). The total power in each lane at game end determines winner. Abilities can boost or reduce power values during the match.

Abilities

Most cards have abilities: Enhance (boost allied cards), Deplete (reduce enemy power), Open (claim new squares), or Convert (flip enemy squares to yours).

Ability Type What It Does Best Used For
Enhance Adds power to adjacent allied cards. Stacking multiple Enhance effects on one card creates enormous power values. Building a dominant single lane
Deplete Reduces power of enemy cards. Can swing a contested lane without playing new cards. Contesting lanes your opponent is winning
Open Claims additional squares beyond the card's pip count. Expands your board control rapidly. Aggressive early board control
Convert Flips enemy-controlled squares to your side. The most powerful lane-swing mechanic in the game. Late-game lane steals, countering dominant setups
Chain Triggers additional effects when played next to specific card types. Combo-based power generation. High-ceiling combo decks

Counter Play

Beat Every Archetype

No single deck wins everything. Swap lists when you hit Convert-heavy tournament NPCs, buff stacks, or Chain combos. Phase gates matter — don't run Seraph Sweep until you have Sephiroth and Bahamut.
Ch. 8+ Unlock

The Convert Trump — Sephiroth

Tournament Reward · S Tier

Unlocked after the Gold Saucer Queen's Blood tournament. Convert flips an entire lane to your side — the single strongest swing in the game. Every late deck builds around baiting the opponent into over-committing, then converting their stacked lane on round 3. You cannot run Seraph Sweep until you have this card.

Card Rankings

Card Tier List

Note on Tiers: Queen's Blood is a context-sensitive game — a C-tier card in one matchup can be game-winning in another. These ratings reflect general-purpose value across most matchups and deck archetypes.
Sephiroth
PIPS 3 POWER 15 S Tier
Convert: Flips all cards in one lane to your side. The most powerful ability in the game. Win condition on its own — forces opponents to play around it every match.
Bahamut
PIPS 3 POWER 13 S Tier
Enhance All: Boosts all allied cards in the same lane by +4 power each. Playing Bahamut on a lane with 2 other cards in it can add 8+ total power instantly. Wins lanes outright.
Cactuar
PIPS 1 POWER 2 S Tier
Open (3): Claims 3 additional squares when placed. Exceptional board control for a 1-pip card. The best early-game play in the game — locks down center squares before your opponent can respond.
Aerith
PIPS 2 POWER 7 A Tier
Enhance Adjacent (×2): Doubles the power of all adjacent allied cards. Placed correctly between two other cards, the power swing can be 10–20. Essential in power-stacking decks.
Tifa
PIPS 2 POWER 9 A Tier
Enhance Column: Adds +3 to every allied card in her column. High base power and a column-wide buff makes her excellent in any lane-dominant deck. One of the best 2-pip cards available.
Titan
PIPS 2 POWER 11 A Tier
Stomp: Depletes all enemy cards in adjacent squares by −5. Exceptional lane-clearing tool. Play Titan next to an opponent's stacked lane to completely neutralize their power advantage.
Chocobo & Mog
PIPS 1 POWER 3 A Tier
Chain (Summon): When played next to a Summon card, both cards gain +3 power. Pairs perfectly with Ifrit or Bahamut. A cheap card that can punch well above its cost in Summon-heavy decks.
Tonberry
PIPS 1 POWER 4 B Tier
Chef's Knife: Depletes the single highest-power enemy card on the board by −8. Niche but devastating against power-inflation decks. Keep 1–2 copies to counter Bahamut/Aerith stacks.
Mandragora
PIPS 1 POWER 3 B Tier
Screech: Enhances all allied 1-pip cards on the board by +2. Cheap filler that rewards running a 1-pip heavy deck. Gets better the more 1-pip cards you've already placed.
Ifrit
PIPS 2 POWER 8 B Tier
Hellfire: Depletes all enemy cards in a column by −3. Area Deplete is powerful for contesting wide lanes. Solid power base for a 2-pip. Good synergy with Chocobo & Mog.
Goblin
PIPS 1 POWER 1 C Tier
Punch: Deals −1 to an adjacent enemy card. Extremely cheap — useful only for filling board squares and triggering Chain effects from other cards. Don't build around this; use it as filler.
Leviathan
PIPS 3 POWER 10 C Tier
Tidal Wave: Depletes all enemy cards by −2. Sounds good but the effect is too spread out to swing lanes efficiently. Bahamut or Sephiroth are better 3-pip investments. Leviathan is a budget substitute only.
NPC Opponents

How to Beat Key Opponents

Sidequest: Playing Queen's Blood against NPCs throughout the world earns new cards, Gil, and story beats. The final Queen's Blood tournament is a required story sequence. Defeat every opponent you find — many give unique cards unavailable elsewhere.
Early Game Opponents
Kalm Villager Tutorial-level opponent. Plays random 1-pip cards with no strategy. Win by: establishing center lane with Cactuar on turn 1, then filling the other two lanes with anything. Should be a clean sweep.
Junon Soldier Runs a basic power deck. Tends to pile cards into the center lane early. Win by: conceding center, building dominant power in both side lanes with Aerith + Tifa. Win 2-1 is fine.
Cargo Ship Crew First opponent who tries to use Deplete effects. Win by: spreading your high-power cards across all three lanes rather than concentrating. His Deplete cards only hit one target — don't give him a single high-value target to nuke.
Mid Game Opponents
Gold Saucer Champion Uses a Convert-heavy deck. Will try to flip your lanes in round 3. Win by: building such an overwhelming power lead in two lanes that even after converts you stay ahead. Do NOT spread thin — dominate 2 lanes hard and abandon the third entirely.
Cosmo Canyon Scholar Runs a Chain combo deck with Summon synergies. Dangerous in round 2 onward. Win by: attacking early before his chains activate. Rush center lane turns 1–2 with Open cards. If he can't place early summons, his chain deck falls apart.
Gongaga Merchant Balanced deck, nothing special. Win by: standard play. Center control turn 1, side lanes turns 2–3. This is a card reward fight — just beat him to get the rare card he drops.
Late Game / Tournament
Queen's Blood Tournament — Round 1 Opponent uses a high-pip power deck. Win by: Cactuar center control round 1, Sephiroth ready as the lane-flip trump. Don't telegraph Sephiroth early — wait for them to commit power to a lane, then Convert it.
Queen's Blood Tournament — Final The toughest NPC in the game. Runs a near-optimal deck with Bahamut, Convert, and Chain synergies. Win by: forcing them to react to you. Play Cactuar and Chocobo & Mog aggressively turn 1 to claim maximum board squares. Bait their Bahamut into a lane you're willing to lose, then win the other two with Aerith-enhanced power stacks + Sephiroth Convert on the final round.
