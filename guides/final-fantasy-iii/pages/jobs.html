The Classes

The Job System

FF III's job-change system is the original. Any of your four heroes can become any unlocked job — the art is reading the dungeon ahead and retraining to answer it, while spending your Capacity Points wisely.

01 · How It Works

Jobs, Capacity Points & job levels

  • Crystals unlock jobs in four waves. Everyone starts as an Onion Knight; each Crystal you awaken adds a batch of jobs you can freely assign to any hero.
  • Changing jobs costs Capacity Points (CP), earned from battles. Bigger "distance" between the old and new job costs more — so plan retrains rather than flipping constantly.
  • Job level builds with use and improves that job's effectiveness (and a few abilities/equip options). A freshly-changed job has a short proficiency dip before it settles in.
  • Stats follow the job. Physical jobs raise Strength/Vitality, mage jobs raise the relevant Magic stats — so a long-term caster wants to live in caster jobs.
Pixel Remaster noteThe PR added a job-level boost that greatly eases the Famicom original's harsh job-change penalty — experimenting between jobs is far more forgiving here. This is the 2D Famicom game (the original nameless Light Warriors and the classic Onion Knight), not the DS 3D remake.
