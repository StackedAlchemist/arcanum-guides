Onion Knight, Missables & Tips

The little things that decide an FF III run: the truth about the Onion Knight, how to survive the save-less finale, and the habits that keep a flexible party strong.

08 · The Legend

The Onion Knight

The Onion Knight is your starting job and, for most of the game, the weakest class — its stats and gear lag badly through the mid-game. But it has the highest growth ceiling in the game: equipped with the full Onion gear set and pushed to very high levels, it surpasses even the Ninja and Sage, with near-unbreakable defense and top-tier attack.

The catch is the Onion equipment is extremely rare (tied to very low-rate drops from the toughest optional encounters). It's a dedicated grind for bragging rights and superboss-tier power — not something a normal playthrough needs. If you're not chasing it, just retire the Onion Knight early.

09 · Don't Miss

Missables & prep

  • The no-save finale. The biggest "gotcha" in FF III — the Crystal Tower → Eureka → World of Darkness → Cloud of Darkness run has no save point. Over-level, finalize jobs/gear, and stock Elixirs and Phoenix Downs before you enter.
  • Clear Eureka first. Its legendary weapons (Excalibur, Ragnarok, Masamune) and top spells dramatically upgrade your finale party — grab them before the World of Darkness.
  • Mini / Toad caves. A few dungeons require you to shrink (Mini) or become a Toad to enter — keep the spells handy and avoid extra fights while transformed.
  • Don't burn CP flipping jobs. Retrain with purpose; the PR's job-level boost helps, but Capacity Points are still a resource.
10 · Field Notes

Durable tips

  • Keep casters in caster jobs. Stats grow with the job you live in, so a long-term mage shouldn't moonlight as a Warrior.
  • Always field healing. White Mage early, Devout/Sage late — never run a full party of attackers into a dungeon.
  • Read the dungeon, then retrain. FF III rewards picking jobs for what's ahead (Dragoons before Garuda, a Scholar before Hein).
  • Bows and magic ignore row — put Rangers and mages in the back to take half physical damage for free.
  • Dark Knight for anything that clones. Souleater clears duplicating enemies before they snowball.
  • Save often outside the finale — and once more right before committing to the Crystal Tower.
