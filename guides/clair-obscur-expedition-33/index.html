Clair Obscur: Expedition 33

A turn-based RPG with real-time defense: every enemy attack can be dodged or parried on timing, and every turn is a chance to build a status engine — Burn, Mark, Stains, Perfection — that turns a boss health bar into a delete button. There are no classes. Your build is weapon scaling + active Skills + Pictos and their Luminas.

Level cap is 99. Parry well, and no health bar is too big.

01 · The Loop

Core combat loop

Combat is turn-based on the offense and real-time on the defense. On your turn you spend AP on Skills and basic attacks. On the enemy's turn you must Dodge (easier, repositions) or Parry (tighter timing, opens huge counters and builds resources). Mastering parry is the single biggest skill jump in the game — it both keeps you alive and fuels your offense.

The winning pattern: stack a status mechanic (Burn / Mark / Stains / Perfection) across turns, then trigger a payoff Skill that consumes or multiplies that stack for enormous burst. Optimized parties erase bosses in a single cycle.

  • Learn to parry, not just dodge. Parries open counterattacks and feed resources.
  • AP generation is king. Most top builds are really about getting more turns and more AP per turn.
  • Rotate Pictos to master them — it's how you spread power across the whole team.
02 · The Build System

Pictos & Luminas

This is the class system. Two linked concepts:

  • Pictos — equippable items. Each gives stat bonuses + one passive effect. You can equip only 3 Pictos per character.
  • Luminas — use a Picto for 4 battles to master it, permanently unlocking its passive as a Lumina any character can activate by spending Lumina Points (a shared pool).
Hoarding and mastering Pictos early is the single best habit in the game. Three Pictos each, four fights each, and suddenly you have nine passives to mix across the roster.

04 · The Roster

The six Expeditioners

All six join by mid-Act 2. Active party is 3; reserves swap in if a member is KO'd.

Maelle — Burn carry / one-shot potential

Stances, especially Virtuose Stance, skyrocket her damage — triggered by mechanics like dropping to low HP (with At Death's Door) or her stance flow. Best-in-slot weapon: Medalum, which pushes massive Burn via Pyrolyse (up to ~60 Burn in one action upgraded). The premier delete button; with Burn-crit Pictos stacked, she one-shots much of the game.

Verso — Perfection rank carry / Mark

Perfection rank (D → S): landing hits, dodges, and parries raises rank; getting hit drops it. Higher rank = more damage and stronger Skills, and applies Mark to amplify team damage. Simoso (late, from Simon in The Abyss) accelerates rank and amplifies follow-ups on Marked targets; Dualiso grants extra turns. Attributes: Agility > Luck. Maintains S-rank Mark uptime so Maelle and Lune delete.

Lune — Elemental mage / top healer

Generates and consumes Elemental Stains (Fire/Ice/Lightning/Earth) for boosted Skills and to exploit Weakness/Resistance. Key Skills: Thunderfall (early DPS), Tree of Life (cleanse + party heal), Mayhem (consume all Stains for a nuke), Elemental Trick → Elemental Genesis (crit generates 4 Stains, then huge AoE), Wildfire (3 Burn stacks). Pictos: Critical Burn, Might. Flexible elemental damage + the best healing in the game.

Sciel — Dark damage / AP acceleration / support

Card-based; applies Mark ("Marking Card") and boosts allies ("Fortune's Fury" doubles a partner's damage). Fast rotations, Dark element. Support/enabler that sets up the carry's delete turn.

Monoco — flexible / anti-Break specialist

Adapts to learned enemy abilities; strong against enemies with large Break bars or high resistances. Swap him in for fights where Break or resistance is the wall.

Gustave — early protector / Overcharge

Builds Overcharge for big payoff hits; pairs with Defenceless-style setups for Overcharge crits (story-gated availability). A dependable early-game frontliner and burst.

05 · The Machines

Status engines

Most top builds are really one of these four engines dressed up with different characters:

  • Burn — stack Burn (Maelle/Lune), then convert it to guaranteed crit damage via Critical Burn / Double Burn / Burn Affinity. Turns DoT into burst.
  • Mark — Verso/Sciel apply Mark; the carry's hit on a Marked target is amplified. The team's damage multiplier.
  • Stains — Lune generates elemental Stains, then spends them on Mayhem / Elemental Genesis for element-matched nukes.
  • Perfection — Verso's rank ladder; play clean (hit/dodge/parry, don't get hit) to reach S and unlock his biggest Skills.
The art of the game is layering these: e.g. Verso holds S-rank Mark → Maelle stacks Burn → fires a Burn-crit payoff into a Marked target for tens of millions.

07 · Delete Buttons

Build archetypes

Three proven "delete the boss" patterns:

1 · Gage Master / Last Chance burst (Maelle)

Sciel Marks (Marking Card) and doubles damage (Fortune's Fury) → on Maelle's turn use Last Chance to drop her to 1 HP → triggers Virtuose Stance + At Death's Door → with Eskim (consumes extra AP for damage) + the Roulette Picto (risky multiplier), her hit becomes a literal delete button. Even with sub-optimal gear it does tens of millions.

2 · Fire Paints (Burn)

Stack Burn hard with Maelle/Lune, then convert it to instant burst with Burn-crit Pictos. DoT-as-burst.

3 · Dark Lightning (multi-hit chaos)

Sciel Dark + Lune lightning multi-hits for chaotic chip that snowballs through crit generation.

All three prove the same point: with the right Lumina + Pictos + weapon perk, no health bar is too big.
08 · Progression

Game-phase progression

  • Early: prioritize learning parry timing and mastering Pictos for Luminas. Grab Critical Burn early (available early, fuels Burn builds). Lune's Thunderfall + Tree of Life carry survivability.
  • Mid: commit the carry (usually Maelle), build the Burn or Mark engine, spread Cheater / Energy Master Luminas across damage dealers. Sciel/Verso as enablers.
  • Late: chase best-in-slot weapons (Medalum, Simoso, Eskim), pivot toward Painted Power for endgame scaling, and refine the one-cycle burst rotation.
09 · The Walls

Boss & combat tips

  • Parry the heavy hits. Bosses telegraph big attacks; a parry both survives them and feeds your counter economy. Defensive Pictos cover missed timings.
  • Don't ignore defense. Active defense doesn't make survivability stats useless — one clean boss hit can delete a character. Keep enough Vitality/Defense (or At Death's Door discipline) to absorb a missed parry.
  • Exploit Weakness / Resistance with Lune's Stains and element-matched Skills.
  • Save the payoff turn. Build the stack (Burn/Mark/rank), then spend it. Firing the delete Skill before the setup is the most common mistake.
10 · Field Notes

Secrets & priorities

  • The 4-battle master rule is the whole game. Equip 3 Pictos, win 4 fights, master them, repeat. Front-load this and your Lumina pool explodes.
  • Give damage Luminas to every damage dealer the moment you master them.
  • Last Chance + At Death's Door is the highest-ceiling combo in the game, but it's all-in: one missed parry can end the character. Highest risk, highest reward.
  • Reserves matter — a KO'd active member is replaced by a reserve, so don't field three glass cannons with no backup.
  • Experiment. There are no classes; the same character supports wildly different engines depending on Pictos. The "best" build is the one whose rotation you execute cleanly.
