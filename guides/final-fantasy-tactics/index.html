Final Fantasy Tactics

The Ivalice Chronicles

A tactical RPG of grid-based, turn-order combat in war-torn Ivalice. Your power comes from the Job system: units earn JP, unlock jobs along a tree, and carry abilities across classes. The depth is in cross-class ability combos. Built for the Enhanced version (v1.5.0) with New Game+ and the Zodiac Compatibility viewer. Spoiler-light on story.

01 · The Grid

Combat fundamentals

Battles play out on a 3D grid with height. Units act in an order set by Speed; on a turn you typically Move and take one Action (attack, ability, or item), in either order.

  • Height and facing. Higher ground boosts physical hit and damage and extends some ranges. Hitting from the side or back raises hit chance and crit; being hit from behind is dangerous. Position constantly.
  • Charge times. Many spells and ranged abilities have a cast delay (see CT), so they land on a later tick. Lead the target.
  • Turn order is a resource. Faster units act more often. Slowing the enemy or speeding yourself is as strong as raw damage.
  • Permadeath rule. A downed unit becomes a Crystal/Treasure after a countdown; if it expires, the unit is gone for good (generics). Revive or retreat before the count runs out.
02 · The Clock

The turn system (CT & Speed)

  • Every tick, each unit's CT (Charge Time) rises by its Speed. At CT 100 the unit acts, then CT resets.
  • Higher Speed = more frequent turns. This is why Speed is the most valuable stat in the game.
  • Spells and charged abilities have their own cast CT: select target, the ability charges, and it resolves on a future tick. Fast units can move, get hit, or die before a slow spell lands — account for the delay and for enemies leaving an AoE.
Because turn frequency compounds, small Speed advantages snowball over a long battle. Builds that stack Speed (or haste themselves) act dramatically more often than the enemy.
03 · The Numbers

Stats explained

StatWhat it does
HP / MPHealth and magic pool.

MA (Magick Attack)Scales spell and magickal-ability damage.
BraveryAffects some physical abilities/reaction triggers; very high/low Bravery has side effects.
FaithScales magick (yours and damage taken from magick). High Faith = stronger but more vulnerable; very low Faith resists magick but can't cast well.

Two big build levers: Faith governs the whole magick economy (a low-Faith unit is a poor caster but a magick-resistant tank), and Bravery interacts with physical reaction abilities and a few class mechanics.

04 · The Stars

Zodiac Compatibility

Each unit has a zodiac sign, and a hidden compatibility multiplier between attacker and target modifies damage, healing, and accuracy. In the Enhanced version you can now view a unit's sign and compatibility directly from the status screen (a v1.5.0 addition), so you no longer have to guess.

  • Good compatibility boosts damage/recovery/accuracy (to ~125% in the favorable cases shown in-game).
  • Bad compatibility reduces it (to ~75%).
  • Special compatibility (e.g. certain opposite-sign pairings) can swing dramatically high (~150%) or low; non-human units shift the modifier too.
Pair healers and buffers with allies they're compatible with, and aim your hardest hitters at enemies they match well against. It's a quiet multiplier that adds up across a battle.
05 · The Heart

The Job system

Units belong to a Job (class), earn JP (Job Points) by acting, and spend JP to learn that job's abilities. Mastering jobs and meeting prerequisites unlocks new jobs along a branching tree.

  • Each job has its own level (driven by JP) and its own ability list.
  • Advanced jobs require levels in prerequisite jobs (climb from Squire/Chemist into combat and mage lines, then into elite hybrids).
  • A unit's stat growth depends on the job it levels up in — so the class you grind matters for the final build, not just the abilities you learn.
06 · The Real Build

Ability slots

This is the actual build system and where the depth lives. A unit equips:

  • One Job (primary) — its full action set.
  • One Secondary command — the action abilities of any other job you've learned. The cross-class engine: a Knight with Black Magick, a Monk with White Magick.
  • One Reaction — auto-triggers on a condition (counterattack when hit, auto-heal on damage).
  • One Support — a passive (equip heavier weapons, two-hand, boost a stat, dual-wield).
  • One Movement — movement/positioning passive (+Move, +Jump, move through tiles, teleport).
The game is won in these slots. A "Monk" isn't just a Monk; it's a Monk with Math Skill or Punch Art plus the right Reaction/Support/Movement glued on. Build identity comes from the combination, not the primary job alone.
Enhanced"Remove All Equipment" was added to the status screen, and locked job-unlock conditions are now shown clearly — planning these builds is much faster.
07 · The Climb

Job tree and progression

Broad shape of the tree (names follow the Ivalice Chronicles localization):

  • Base: Squire and Chemist — everyone starts here; Squire's JP-up and Chemist's items are foundational.
  • Physical line: Knight (armor, break equipment), Archer (ranged, charge shots), Monk (high HP/PA, healing punches), Thief (steal, speed), Geomancer (terrain attacks), Dragoon/Lancer (jump), Samurai (draw-out elemental hits), Ninja (dual-wield, throw, very high Speed).
  • Magick line: Black Mage (elemental nukes), White Mage (heal/buff), Time Mage (haste/slow/quick, the turn-economy class), Summoner (big AoE MA), Oracle/Mystic (status ailments), Orator/Mediator (recruit, persuade), and the Mathematician/Calculator class.
  • Elite/hybrid: Mystic Knight/specialty hybrids and the unique story jobs.

Climbing the tree is the long-game project: grind prerequisite jobs not only to unlock the next class but to pick up cross-class abilities your final build wants.

08 · The Best

Standout jobs and why

  • Time Mage — Haste, Slow, and Quick weaponize the turn economy. Some of the strongest action economy in the game.
  • Ninja — innate dual-wield and top-tier Speed; a fast attacker and a premier stat-grind class. Throw adds ranged burst.
  • Monk — huge HP and PA, weaponless, with healing and AoE punch arts; strong early and a great secondary on bruisers.
  • Mathematician (Calculator) — the notorious build-breaker: cast certain spells free on every unit matching a numeric condition (Height/CT/Level/EXP). Slow to set up, devastating once online.
  • Orator/Mediator — recruit enemy humans and some monsters; how you grow a roster of special units and rare classes.
  • Samurai / Summoner — top MA and physical-magick burst for clearing groups.
  • Knight — equipment/stat breaks neuter dangerous enemies; sturdy frontline.

The "best" job is always relative to the combination you build around it.

09 · The Loadouts

Build archetypes

  1. Speed-stacked Ninja carry — Ninja primary (dual-wield) + a strong secondary (Black Magick, Punch Art, Draw Out) + a damage Reaction + Two-Hands/Attack-Up + Move+. Acts constantly, hits twice, snowballs the turn order.
  2. Battle-mage Knight — Knight primary (breaks + armor) + Black/White Magick secondary, so one durable unit tanks and casts. Faith management makes it flexible.
  3. Time-mage enabler — Time Mage primary for Haste/Quick on your carry and Slow on the enemy; pair with Short Charge to land control fast. Wins by owning the clock.
  4. Monk bruiser — Monk primary (no weapon needed, high HP/PA) + Punch Art self-sustain + Counter/Hamedo Reaction + Move/Jump; a self-healing wrecking ball.
  5. Calculator nuke — Mathematician primary with Black/White/Holy secondary; set Height/CT/Level/EXP conditions to blanket the field with free spells. The endgame "delete everything" build — slow but absurd.
  6. Archer / positional sniper — high ground + charge shots + Concentrate accuracy; punishes from range and abuses height/facing.
The throughline: lock the turn economy and the cross-class secondary first, then bolt on Reaction/Support/Movement to cover the build's weakness.
10 · The Named

Special / story units

Story and recruited characters bring unique jobs and high ceilings: Ramza (a buffed Squire line with powerful self/party buffs), and a roster of named guests with exclusive classes you pick up across the campaign. These units are typically stronger than generics and can't be permanently lost the way generics can. Build them as your core, and use generics to grind prerequisite jobs and fill roles.

12 · Field Notes

General strategy

  • Speed is king. When you can choose a stat to stack or a class to grind for growth, Speed compounds harder than anything else.
  • Win the turn economy with Haste/Quick/Slow before trying to out-damage a tough fight.
  • Abuse height and facing every turn: attack from behind/side, hold high ground, force the enemy to climb.
  • Manage Faith deliberately. High Faith = strong casting but fragile to magick; low Faith = magick-resistant tanks who can't cast. Build each unit to one side.
  • Grind prerequisite jobs on purpose — to unlock elite classes and to harvest cross-class abilities for the secondary/reaction/support/movement slots.
  • Protect downed units. Revive or retreat before the Crystal countdown ends, especially for JP-invested generics.
  • Use Zodiac Compatibility now that it's visible: a favorable matchup is a free ~25% swing on damage, healing, and accuracy.
  • Use New Game+ to push harder builds and rarer class setups on a second run with levels and items intact.
