The Craft

Building a Character

We don't pin "the best build" here — it changes every patch and we'd only mislead you. Instead, this is how a build is assembled and how to read a current meta build off a planner so it actually makes sense when you copy it.

10 · Anatomy

The parts of a build

Every Diablo IV build, however fancy, is the same six layers stacked together. When you read a guide, you're really reading these:

LayerWhat it decides
Skills & the resource loopYour generator(s), your core spender(s), and the buffs/defensives around them — and how you stay resource-positive while using them.
Skill variants & class mechanicThe skill-tree branches, passives, and the class mechanic setup (Enchantments, Book of the Dead, Oath, Warlock archetype, etc.) that the build is built around. LoH removed standalone Key Passives.
Aspects & UniquesThe one or two powers that make the build — usually a damage multiplier or a loop enabler. The rest is support.
Affixes & TemperingThe 3–4 stats every piece should chase, plus the tempered affixes that scale the build's core skill.
Paragon boards & GlyphsWhich boards, the path through them, and which Glyphs to socket and level — a big chunk of total power.
Gems, Runewords & consumablesThe finishing layer: gem stats, a runeword effect, and elixirs/incense for the last few percent.
Find the multiplier. Most builds have one core interaction that multiplies damage (a key Aspect/Unique + a passive + a Tempering affix all feeding one skill). Understand that engine and the rest of the build — defenses, resource, mobility — exists only to keep that engine running.
11 · Two Builds

Leveling vs. endgame

  • Level with whatever clears fast. While leveling, gear changes constantly — don't over-invest. Use a strong, cheap leveling skill and respec freely; it's instant and cheap in town.
  • Your endgame build comes online with gear, usually around level 60–70 once you reach Torment and start collecting Ancestral items and the build's key Uniques/Aspects. Many builds can't function until those pieces exist — that's normal.
  • Respec is not precious. Skills, Paragon and the Enchantment/Oath/etc. slots can all be redone. Experiment; you can't brick a character.
  • Don't fully finish gear you'll replace. Save heavy Masterworking and rare materials for the items you've confirmed are your endgame keepers.
12 · The Meta

How to read & pick a build

Tier lists are perishable — a single patch can promote or gut a build. So judge a build by what you need it for, not just its letter grade:

  • Clear speed — for farming Helltides, Whispers and low Pit tiers fast.
  • Boss / single-target damage — for pushing the Pit and killing tough uniques.
  • Survivability — how forgiving it is at high Torment (do you melt, or face-tank?).
  • Gear dependence — does it need a Mythic Unique to function, or come online cheaply? Great for a fresh season vs. a finished character.

Pick a build whose strengths match your goal and whose gear requirements match where you are. A "B-tier" build you can actually assemble beats an "S-tier" one gated behind an item you don't have.
