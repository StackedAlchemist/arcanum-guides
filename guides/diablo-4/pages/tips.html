Field Notes

Tips, Season Start & Story

The habits that make a Diablo IV run efficient instead of aimless — how to open a new season, how to level fast, the mistakes that quietly waste your time, and a spoiler-gated recap of the story so far.

13 · Day One

The season-start routine

  1. Make a Seasonal character. Seasonal progress is separate — that's where the new mechanic, Battle Pass and Season Journey live. (Your old heroes wait on the Eternal realm.)
  2. Skip the campaign (once you've beaten it on any character) and pick a strong leveling build for your class.
  3. Engage the season mechanic early — it's usually free power and the point of the season; the Season Journey guides you through it.
  4. Blast to max level via Helltides, Legion events and Whispers, then unlock Torment.
  5. Pivot to your endgame build as Ancestral gear and your key Aspects/Uniques come in, then start the gear-and-Paragon loop.
14 · Speed

Leveling fast

  • Helltides are the engine — dense packs, good XP, and targeted loot. Live in them while leveling.
  • Group up. Party play and Legion/World events stack mobs and multiply XP.
  • Use XP boosts — Urns of Aggression, incense, and elixirs add up over a leveling session.
  • Don't read every drop. While leveling, grab obvious upgrades and salvage the rest for materials — you'll replace everything by endgame anyway.
  • Respec freely to keep your clear speed high as new skills unlock; it's cheap and instant.
15 · Pitfalls

Common mistakes

  • Ignoring defenses. Cap your Resistances and hit the Armor cap before pushing Torment — you die to scaling damage, not lack of DPS.
  • Pushing difficulty too fast. If you're dying repeatedly, you farm slower. Stay one tier below comfortable and climb when it's trivial.
  • Over-finishing temporary gear. Don't dump Masterworking mats and rare materials into items you'll replace — save them for confirmed keepers.
  • Forgetting the Codex. Extract good Aspects (or unlock them from dungeons) so you can re-imprint your build's key powers onto fresh drops.
  • Farming the wrong activity. Know what you need — Glyph XP, Masterworking mats, or a specific slot — and run the activity that drops it.
  • Skipping the season mechanic. It's usually significant power; treating it as optional leaves you weaker than the content expects.
16 · The Story

What it's all about

⚠ Spoiler — the story through both expansions

Base campaign: cultists summon Lilith, daughter of the Prime Evil Mephisto and co-creator of humanity, back to Sanctuary. She means to empower mortals with demonic blood to survive the eternal war between the Hells and the Heavens — by any brutal means. Alongside Lorath and the young mage Neyrelle, and the fallen angel Inarius (Lilith's former lover, who opposes her), you hunt her down and kill Lilith at the gates of Hell. But it's a hollow victory: the real schemer is Mephisto, who manipulated everyone. Neyrelle traps Mephisto's essence in a soulstone and walks off carrying that terrible burden alone.

Vessel of Hatred: Neyrelle carries the soulstone to the jungles of Nahantu, slowly poisoned by Mephisto's whispers; you follow to help her resist him while the Lord of Hatred works to break free and turn the region's faith to his ends.

Lord of Hatred: the conflict escalates to the Skovos Isles, where Mephisto — now ascendant — wages a crusade, and new champions (the Paladin and Warlock) are drawn into the fight to stop the Prime Evil's plan. The throughline of the whole saga is simple and grim: this is Mephisto's story, and hatred is winning.
