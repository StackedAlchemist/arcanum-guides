The Eight

Classes

Eight classes, each defined by a resource and one signature mechanic that shapes everything it does. Pick by the fantasy and feel you want — every class can reach the top tiers of content with the right build.

02 · The Mechanics

The signature systems, explained

  • Barbarian — Arsenal. You carry four weapons (two one-handed, a two-handed bludgeon, a two-handed slashing) and skills are tied to weapon types, so you cycle gear constantly and benefit from Weapon Expertise bonuses on each.
  • Sorcerer — Enchantments. Slot a skill into an Enchantment to gain its effect passively instead of casting it — e.g. Fireball that explodes corpses, or Teleport on Evade. Build-defining flexibility.
  • Druid — Shapeshift & Boons. Flow between human, Werebear (tanky/earth) and Werewolf (fast/storm) forms; at the Spirit Hall you pick Spirit Boons from four animal spirits for permanent buffs.
  • Rogue — Specialization & Imbuements. Choose Combo Points (spenders scale with built points), Inner Sight (free unlimited spenders on a meter), or Preparation (cooldown engine) — then layer Poison / Cold / Shadow Imbuements onto attacks.
  • Necromancer — Book of the Dead. Customize each minion type (Skeletal Warriors, Mages, Golem) or sacrifice a type for a permanent personal buff — the choice between army and solo caster.
  • Spiritborn — Spirit Guardians. Jaguar (ferocity/burn damage), Eagle (mobility/crit), Gorilla (defense/barrier), Centipede (poison/sustain). Your primary and secondary guardian choices drive the whole build.
  • Paladin — Oaths. Swear an Oath to lock in an archetype (sturdy frontline, holy nuker, or relentless zeal); Faith is generated by basic skills and spent on powerful holy abilities.
  • Warlock — Soul Shards. A dual economy: build Wrath for spells and Dominance to summon and command a Greater Demon. Your Soul Shard sets the archetype and which demon you call; Soul Fragments tune how it behaves.
03 · The Pick

Which class is for you

  • Want to be tanky and never die? Barbarian or Paladin — armor, shouts/oaths, and heavy hits.
  • Want to nuke from range? Sorcerer or Warlock — elemental and demonic spellcasting.
  • Want an army fighting for you? Necromancer (minions) or Warlock (Greater Demon) — let the summons do the work.
  • Want speed and flow? Rogue or Spiritborn — fast, mobile, combo-driven clears.
  • Want versatility in one body? Druid shifts between tank, caster and companion builds more than any other class.
Don't overthink the first pick. Respeccing skills and Paragon is cheap and instant in town, and every class has multiple viable endgame builds. Choose the fantasy you'll enjoy for 60+ levels; the optimal build comes later (see Builds). Spiritborn, Paladin and Warlock require their expansions.
