The Long Road

Hunts, Bosses & Endgame

Once the road opens, XV's real challenges are optional: the hunt board, the brutal secret dungeons, the colossal Adamantoise, and the Royal Edition's new bosses. Here's what's out there, how to prepare, and what the DLC adds. Story-boss specifics are kept behind spoilers.

01 · The board

Hunts

  • Pick them up at diners. Talk to tipsters in restaurants to accept hunts — bounties on a specific monster in a set area (and sometimes time of day).
  • Star-ranked. Each hunt has a difficulty rating; clearing them raises your Hunter rank, which unlocks tougher, better-paying hunts.
  • The reliable income. Hunts pay gil, AP and EXP, and drop rare ingredients and materials for Cid's weapon upgrades and Ignis's best meals. They're the backbone of mid-game progression.
  • Daemons come at night. Many hunt targets (and the toughest roaming enemies) only appear after dark — powerful, but the night is where the XP and rare drops live.
02 · Story bosses

The campaign's big fights

The main-story bosses are mostly spectacle set-pieces — read the telegraph, phase the big hits, and use Ignis's Enhancement on the right element. The named fights spoil the plot, so they live behind the toggle.

⚠ Spoiler — major story bosses & the late chapters

The Astral trials — the bound fights to earn Titan, Ramuh and Leviathan are scripted spectacles; follow the prompts and survive the barrages.

Chapter 13 strips Noctis of his weapons for a stretch and leans on the Ring of the Lucii — the Royal Edition reworked this chapter to be far less punishing, with Armiger access restored. Lean on the ring's Death/Holy.

The finale is a multi-phase duel that's more emotional beat than mechanical wall — come with potions and a strong main weapon and it resolves cleanly.

After the credits you're returned to the open world to mop up everything below.

03 · Into the dark

Secret & menace dungeons

The toughest content sits underground. Several normal dungeons hide a sealed door to a menace dungeon, and a few standalone ruins are challenges all their own.

DungeonWhat it is
Costlemark TowerA puzzle-box dungeon of shifting paths and Red Giants; hides a menace-dungeon door. Bring patience and AoE.
Steyliff GroveOriginally a timed expedition; a long descent with strong daemons and good loot.
The Menace dungeons (8)Post-game extensions beneath existing dungeons — escalating enemy levels and unique bosses, ending in very high-level fights and top gear/accessories.
Pitioss RuinsA pure platforming gauntlet — almost no combat, fiendish navigation, reached by the flying Regalia. Rewards include the auto-evade Black Hood and unique gear. A patience test, not a damage test.
Gear up first. Menace bosses can dwarf the story's level range. Bring lifesteal accessories, an Expericast-leveled Noctis, a Cid-upgraded main weapon, and the Ring of the Lucii for the high-defense fights.
04 · The big one

The Adamantoise

The mountain-sized turtle is XV's signature mega-hunt — an enormous health pool that turns into a marathon. Two ways to handle it:

  • Ring of the Lucii — Death. Channelling Death drains its HP and ignores its defense, cutting the fight dramatically. The classic method.
  • Buff and grind. A max-HP/strength meal, a strong weapon and patience also gets there — just expect a long fight. Hit the head/eyes when it lowers them.
05 · The complete edition

Royal / Windows Edition extras

  • Explore Insomnia. The Royal Edition expands the final approach into the Crown City as a free-roam area with new bosses — including Cerberus and the superboss Omega.
  • Armiger Unleashed. The free-form Armiger combo (see Combat) and a reworked, more playable Chapter 13.
  • The Royal Vessel & Regalia Type-D/F. Free-roam the boat, take the car off-road (Type-D), and eventually fly it (Type-F) to reach Pitioss and far corners.
  • More Royal Arms content and quality-of-life additions folded into the base experience.
06 · Add-ons

The DLC episodes

XV's add-ons each spotlight a character with their own gameplay twist:

EpisodeAdds
Episode GladiolusPlay Gladio in a combo-focused side story with a stamina/valor mechanic.
Episode PromptoA gunplay-and-snowmobile chapter from Prompto's perspective.
Episode IgnisIgnis's tale — a tight action moveset and an alternate-ending "what if."
Episode ArdynPlay the antagonist in the back-story finale to the DLC arc.
ComradesThe online multiplayer expansion — create an avatar and take co-op hunts.
Edition noteWhich episodes are bundled depends on your edition and platform — some were sold separately and availability shifted over the game's life. Check what's installed before planning a 100% run.
