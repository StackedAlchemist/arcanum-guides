The Build Tree

Ascension, Levels & Camp

XV splits "getting stronger" into three systems: Ascension (spend AP on a skill grid), levels (earn EXP, but only bank it when you rest), and cooking (Ignis's meals are real, stacking buffs). Use all three deliberately and Noctis outscales the curve fast.

01 · The grid

Ascension constellations

You spend AP on nodes across several constellations. AP is separate from EXP — it comes from how you fight and explore, not from kills alone.

First AP to spend: the Exploration and Teamwork AP-gain nodes (so everything earns more AP), then warp-strike Combat upgrades, then the Technique bar boosts. With AP-gain nodes bought early, the whole grid fills itself over a playthrough.
02 · Where AP comes from

Farming AP

  • Skilful combat. Warp-kills, parries, link-strikes and finishing without items all grant AP — play stylishly, not just safely.
  • Exploration activities. Riding a chocobo, fishing, and snapping photos all feed AP once you own the matching nodes.
  • Quests & hunts. Side quests and hunts pay AP alongside gil and EXP.
  • The snowball: buy AP-gain nodes first and every later activity returns more — AP is rarely a bottleneck once you do.
03 · Levels

EXP banks when you rest

This trips up new players: combat and quests give EXP, but it sits as pending until you sleep. Where you sleep multiplies the whole pending pile — so a big day of EXP cashed in at a luxury suite is worth far more than the same EXP slept off at a free camp.

Where you restEXP multiplierNotes
Haven (camp)×1.0Free, and the only place Ignis cooks. No EXP bonus, but the meal buff is the point.
Caravans / budget motels~×1.5Cheap gil for a modest boost.
Hotels~×2.0The standard "bank a big day" option.
Luxury suitesup to ×3.0e.g. Galdin Quay's premier suite. Hoard pending EXP, then cash out here.
The leveling playDon't sleep after every fight. Stack pending EXP across a hunt-heavy day, then drive to a ×3 suite and bank it all at once. Stacking an EXP-bonus meal on top compounds it further. (Exact multipliers vary by lodging — verify in-game.) For the heavy postgame grind — Expericast and the coin trick — see Hunts & Endgame.
04 · Camp

Ignis's cooking

  • Meals are buffs. Rest at a haven and Ignis cooks a dish granting effects that last until your next meal/rest — bonus max HP, strength, magic, resistances, or an EXP bonus. Pick the meal for the fight ahead.
  • Ignis levels up his cooking as you eat at restaurants, gather ingredients and find recipes — "I've come up with a new recipe!" unlocks progressively stronger dishes.
  • Restaurants vs camp. Town restaurants serve buff meals without resting; camping is free and required for Ignis's home cooking. Eat a big buff before a boss or a long hunt.
  • Ingredients matter. Many top dishes need specific ingredients from hunts, vendors and fishing — another reason to do the side content.
