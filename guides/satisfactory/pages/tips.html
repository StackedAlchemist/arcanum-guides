Tips & Secrets

The systems the tutorial waves at but never explains — the ones that quietly decide whether your run takes 40 hours or 120. Hard Drives, the MAM trees, the AWESOME Sink, and the exploration loot worth detouring for.

Hard Drives & alternate recipes

Hard Drives are the single biggest efficiency lever in the game. Found in crash sites (downed FICSIT drop pods) scattered across the map, each one is analyzed in the MAM and offers a choice of alternate recipes.

  • You pick 1 of up to 3 offered per drive — and you can re-roll the options later if none fit, so grab the best now and revisit.
  • Alternates can dramatically cut inputs. Recipes like Cast Screw, Steel Rotor, Stitched Iron Plate, Coated Cable, and Solid Steel Ingot remove whole bottleneck steps.
  • Fuel/power alternates are gold: the Turbofuel and Compacted Coal chains massively improve power density (see Power).
  • Detour for them. A morning spent with the Explorer collecting drives pays for itself many times over.
Don't chase every alternate

More alternates aren't automatically better — some trade a cheaper input for a more complex supply chain. Pick alternates that remove a step you're already struggling to feed (usually screws or heavy intermediates), not ones that add a new ingredient you don't produce yet.

The AWESOME Sink & Shop

Feed any excess parts into the AWESOME Sink; each part is worth points, and points become Coupons. Spend coupons in the AWESOME Shop.

  • Sink your overflow, always. A factory that backs up stops producing — an overflow belt into the Sink keeps lines running and earns coupons. Free value from waste.
  • Coupon priorities: belt & pipe Mk upgrades, the Blueprint Designer expansions, the Somersloop-free production cosmetics, and storage/QoL unlocks.
  • Sink point value rises the more you sink — sinking higher-tier parts is far more coupon-efficient than raw materials.

Power Slugs & Power Shards

Glowing slugs are scattered across the map (often in caves and on cliffs). Craft them into Power Shards for overclocking:

SlugYieldsWhere
Blue Power Slug1 Power ShardCommon, out in the open.
Yellow Power Slug2 Power ShardsLess common, often elevated.
Purple Power Slug5 Power ShardsRare, usually in caves or hard-to-reach spots.
Where shards earn their keep

Overclock miners on Pure nodes first — one shard there multiplies a whole supply chain. After that, use shards on single-machine bottlenecks you physically can't widen. Remember overclock power cost is exponential (see clock math).

Somersloops & Mercer Spheres

  • Somersloops are alien artifacts that, slotted into a production building, double that machine's output (production amplification) for extra power — or feed Power Augmenters to boost your whole grid. They're finite and precious; put them on your most expensive end products (computers, fuel rods, project parts), never on cheap base parts.
  • Mercer Spheres unlock and upgrade the Dimensional Depot — upload parts once and withdraw them from anywhere on the map. Game-changing for keeping a build kit (foundations, belts, basic parts) always on hand without hauling.
  • Both are collectibles tied to the Alien Technology MAM tree — grab every one you pass.
  • SPWN (1.2) — the movable spawn point. Unlocked in the Alien Technology MAM tree, it lets you reset where you respawn so you're not hiking back across the map after a death. Costs 1 Mercer Sphere, 11 SAM Fluctuators, 20 Steel Pipe — one of the better single uses of a spare Mercer Sphere.

Quality-of-life you should turn on early

  • Blueprint Designer. Build a module once, save it, then stamp copies instantly. The biggest time-saver in the game — design your standard "smelter block" or "RIP block" and never hand-place it again.
  • Dimensional Depot. Once unlocked, keep construction staples uploaded so you can build anywhere without a backpack full of foundations.
  • Customizer (paint & patterns). Color-code factories by product — it's not just cosmetic, it makes a sprawling base navigable.
  • Priority Power Switches. Segment non-critical loads so a deficit sheds the right things instead of blacking out the base (see Power).
  • 1.2 vehicle paths via Build Gun. You no longer have to manually drive routes — lay vehicle paths directly. Re-do any old hand-driven routes that were imperfect.
