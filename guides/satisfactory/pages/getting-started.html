The Onboarding

Getting Started

Drop pod's down, the HUB's unpacked, and the planet is enormous. This is the path that gets a brand-new pioneer from "what do I do" to a self-running coal grid and a steady Space Elevator feed — without the mistakes you'd otherwise tear down later.

Choosing a landing zone

Four starting areas. They change difficulty, not the goal — you can reach anywhere on the map eventually.

ZoneDifficultyCharacter
Grass FieldsEasiestOpen, flat, resource-dense, central. The recommended first-playthrough start.
Rocky DesertMediumGood resources, more vertical terrain. Scenic and very buildable.
Northern ForestMediumDense cover, decent resources, tighter building space.
Dune DesertHardest startSparse early resources and big open distances. For players who want a challenge.
If you randomized your world (1.2)

With Random resource settings, your starting zone's usual ores may be different. Before committing a base, scout outward on foot and confirm you have iron, copper, limestone, and a coal source within belt/truck reach. Keep a few Biomass Burners running longer than usual until you've found and automated coal.

The first hour, in order

  1. Hand-mine iron and feed the HUB to unlock the first upgrades. Don't overthink it — you'll relocate this later.
  2. Build a portable miner + a couple of Smelters on an iron node. Automating iron ingots is the first real milestone.
  3. Set up the core early line: Iron Plates and Iron Rods, then Screws. These three feed almost everything in Tiers 0–2.
  4. Automate Solid Biofuel so your Biomass Burners stop needing hand-feeding. Power should never require babysitting.
  5. Build Reinforced Iron Plates (30 Plate + 60 Screw → 5 RIP). This is the first "assembler" bottleneck and the gateway to Smart Plating for the Space Elevator. See Ratios.
  6. Rush Coal Power. The single biggest early quality-of-life jump. Build the 3 Water Extractor : 8 Coal Generator block and never hand-feed power again.
Build to relocate

Your first base is a prototype. Build it functional, not beautiful. Once you understand the map and your ratios, you'll either expand it deliberately or abandon it for a proper main factory — that's normal, not failure.

The milestone path

Tiers unlock at the HUB Terminal by delivering parts; the Space Elevator gates the big phase jumps via Project Assembly parts. A clean priority order:

PhaseGoalWhat it gives you
Tiers 0–2Core parts + Coal PowerStable automated power and the basic part economy (plates, rods, screws, wire, cable, concrete).
Tiers 3–4Steel + LogisticsSteel beams/pipes, Mk.3 belts, trucks, and the Pipeline T-Junction (1.2). Steel opens Modular Frames and real building.
Tier 5Oil + Logistics Mk.4Plastic, rubber, fuel power, and the new Fluid Trucks. Oil is the mid-game inflection point.
Tiers 6–7Computers, faster belts, trainsMk.4/Mk.5 belts, train network, advanced parts. The map starts to feel connected.
Tier 8+Aluminum, then NuclearHeat sinks, batteries, and eventually nuclear power — the endgame production tier.
Unlock the MAM early

The MAM (Molecular Analysis Machine) is where Hard Drives become alternate recipes and where Caterium/Quartz/Sulfur trees live. Don't ignore it — a couple of good alternate recipes can halve a factory's footprint. See Tips & Secrets.

Rookie mistakes to skip

  • Hand-feeding power forever. Automate biofuel, then rush coal. Power is infrastructure, not a chore.
  • Building one machine "to see if it works." Plan the whole block's ratio first, then place all the machines. Half-built lines become spaghetti.
  • Ignoring foundations. Snap everything to foundations from day one. Belts and machines on raw terrain are impossible to align and extend.
  • Hoarding nodes you can't use. Tap what your current line needs at clean ratios; over-mining just backs belts up and wastes miners.
  • Skipping the AWESOME Sink. Build it as soon as it's available — excess parts become coupons for genuinely useful unlocks (see Tips).
  • Never exploring. Hard Drives, Power Slugs, and crash sites are scattered everywhere and dramatically speed up your run.
