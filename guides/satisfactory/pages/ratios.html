The Math

Production Ratios

Every rate below is items-per-minute at 100% clock speed. Build machines in these whole-number groups and your belts saturate exactly — no starved assemblers, no backed-up smelters. Scale a whole block by multiplying every count by the same number.

How to use this page

Find your target part, read the recipe, and read the building count for a clean belt. "Clean belt" means a whole number of machines whose total output lands on a real belt tier (60 / 120 / 270 / 480 / 780 / 1200 per min). Type in the search box to jump to a recipe.

Recipe Cost Multiplier — match your Game Mode

Picked a non-default Recipe Parts Cost Multiplier when you created your save? Set it here and every input amount below rescales to match. Outputs stay the same — you're just feeding more or fewer raws into the same machines.

Oil basics (Refinery)

Oil is where byproduct management starts to matter. The base Plastic recipe leaves Heavy Oil Residue behind — if you don't consume or sink it, the refinery backs up and stops.

OutputRecipe (per min @ 100%)Byproduct & fix
Plastic30 Crude Oil → 20 Plastic + 10 Heavy Oil ResiduePipe the 10 HOR into a Residual Fuel or Residual Rubber refinery, or sink it. Never leave it uncapped.
Rubber30 Crude Oil → 20 Rubber + 20 Heavy Oil ResidueEven more HOR per unit — pair Rubber lines with a Residual Fuel burner.
The clean oil loop

A tidy pattern: run Plastic + Rubber, route all Heavy Oil Residue into Residual Fuel, then burn that Fuel in generators for free power. One crude node can supply plastics, rubber, and a power block if you balance the residue. See Power for the fuel-generator math.

Manifolds, balancers & clock speed

Manifold (recommended)

Run one belt past a row of machines, tapping each with a splitter in a line. Inputs auto-balance once buffers fill.

  • Pro: trivial to build and extend, no math on the belt itself.
  • Con: machines fill left-to-right at first — give it a few minutes to reach steady state.
  • Use for: almost everything. This is the default modern build style.

Load balancer

Use splitters/mergers to split a belt into exactly equal shares so every machine gets identical input instantly.

  • Pro: instant even saturation, no warm-up.
  • Con: fiddly; awkward counts (3, 7) need smart splitters or manifold anyway.
  • Use for: the rare case where warm-up time genuinely matters.

Clock speed & overclocking

  • Output scales linearly with clock speed: 150% clock = 150% output.
  • Power cost scales exponentially (exponent ~1.321). A machine at 250% draws far more than 2.5× the power.
  • Underclocking is the hidden gem: running a machine at 50% costs much less than half the power. Underclock for power-tight builds (and especially under the 1.2 Power Consumption multiplier).
  • Overclock with Power Shards (max 250%). Reserve them for miners on Pure nodes and single-machine bottlenecks where you can't fit more buildings.
Belt tiers to memorize

Mk.1 = 60 · Mk.2 = 120 · Mk.3 = 270 · Mk.4 = 480 · Mk.5 = 780 · Mk.6 = 1200 items/min. Build machine groups whose total output lands on one of these and a single belt carries the whole block.
