The Veins

Logistics & Transport

Production is only half the game; getting parts from A to B at the right rate is the other half. Belts and pipes for short hauls, trains and drones for long ones, and — new in Update 1.2 — Fluid Trucks for moving liquids by road.

Conveyor belts

The default mover for solids. Each tier doubles-ish the throughput; a belt is a hard cap — output beyond its rate simply backs up.

Belt discipline

Plan lines so a full block's output equals one belt tier exactly (see Ratios). When a single belt can't carry the load, split onto two before the bottleneck, not after. Mergers obey belt caps too — three full Mk.3 belts merging onto one Mk.3 belt loses two-thirds of the flow.

Pipelines

For fluids — water, oil, fuel, acids, and aluminum-line liquids. Pipes add a second variable belts don't have: headlift.

PipeThroughputNote
Pipeline Mk.1300 m³/minFine for most early fluid lines.
Pipeline Mk.2600 m³/minFor high-volume fuel and water mains.
  • Headlift: fluids only rise so far on their own. To pump up, add Pipeline Pumps, each adding a fixed lift — stack them on tall vertical runs.
  • Keep mains horizontal. Run pipes flat where possible; reserve vertical climbs for pumped sections to avoid air pockets.
  • Buffer with tanks. A Fluid Buffer near the consumer smooths surges and prevents the last machine in a row from starving.
  • 1.2 added the Pipeline T-Junction (Tier 3) — cleaner three-way splits without awkward junction geometry.

Trains

The long-haul backbone. High throughput, fully automatable, and they cross the map tirelessly.

  • Each Freight Car docks at a Freight Platform that loads/unloads one item type. Chain platforms for multi-cargo stations.
  • Throughput is set by the platform's belt, not the train — feed stations with your best belt tier.
  • Signals matter: use Path Signals at intersections and Block Signals on open track to run multiple trains without deadlock.
  • Trains also haul fluids via Fluid Freight Platforms — an alternative to a cross-map pipeline.
  • Best for: moving large volumes of refined ore or parts between distant production sites.

Drones

  • Point-to-point air freight. A Drone flies between two Drone Ports carrying a fixed cargo load each trip.
  • Powered by Batteries — the route consumes batteries per trip, so budget a battery supply at the home port.
  • Low throughput, zero infrastructure. No track to lay, terrain doesn't matter. Ideal for small quantities of high-value parts (computers, frames, fuel rods) across long distances.
  • Best for: trickle-feeding a remote outpost something it can't make locally.

Vehicles & the new Fluid Trucks (1.2)

Road vehicles — Tractor, Truck, Explorer — follow recorded paths. Update 1.2 rebuilt vehicle pathing entirely: you can now place paths with the Build Gun instead of only driving them manually, the self-driving is smoother, and path colors are customizable in UI settings.

New in Update 1.2 — Fluid Trucks

Liquids can now move by road. Fluid Truck Stations and Fluid Trucks (3200 m³ capacity each) are unlocked at Tier 5 as part of the Logistics Mk.4 milestone. A truck fills at one Fluid Station, drives its path, and empties at another — no pipeline required across the terrain in between.

When to use them: moving crude oil or fuel from a remote field back to base when laying a long pumped pipeline (with headlift over hills) would be a nightmare. The truck doesn't care about elevation.

  • Truck: large inventory, good for bulk solids on a fixed loop near base.
  • Tractor: cheaper, smaller, unlocked earlier — a fine first automated hauler.
  • Explorer: fast scout vehicle for finding nodes, Hard Drives, and slugs (see Tips), not really for freight.
  • Truck Stations auto-load/unload and can refuel the vehicle; keep a fuel supply at the station or vehicles strand mid-route.
