The Grid

Power Progression

Power is the heartbeat. Every generator below is listed with its exact output and fuel draw at 100% clock. Build to the clean ratios and your grid never browns out — the fastest way to lose an hour is a power trip that halts a factory you forgot was on the same line.

Biomass — the bootstrap

Biomass Burners are hand-fed and only a stopgap, but you live on them until coal is automated. Burn the highest-density fuel you can craft:

  • Leaves → Biomass → Solid Biofuel. Each refinement step packs far more energy per slot. Always upgrade your fuel before stuffing burners.
  • Automate the misery away early. The moment you can, build a Constructor making Solid Biofuel and feed burners off a belt instead of by hand.
  • Get off biomass as the #1 priority. Coal generators are the first "set it and forget it" power. Rush the Coal Power milestone.

Coal — your first real grid

Coal Generators are fully automatable and the backbone of the early-mid game.

Output75 MW
Coal / min15
Water / min45 m³
The 3:8 ratio — memorize this

A Water Extractor pumps 120 m³/min. A Coal Generator drinks 45 m³/min. So 3 Water Extractors feed exactly 8 Coal Generators (360 = 360 water). That block produces 600 MW and eats 120 coal/min — precisely one Mk.2 belt of coal. Stamp this block as many times as you need.

Plumbing note: keep generators on a level loop and use a small buffer tank. Pipes that snake up and down create headlift problems and air pockets that starve the last generator in the row.

Fuel — the workhorse of the mid-late game

Once oil is flowing, Fuel Generators carry your grid into the hundreds of megawatts with far fewer buildings than coal.

Free power from waste

Route the Heavy Oil Residue byproduct from your plastic/rubber lines (see Ratios) into Residual Fuel, then burn it. It's power you'd otherwise have to sink. Turbofuel takes this further — convert Fuel + Compacted Coal into Turbofuel and your fuel-per-MW plummets.

Geothermal — free but fixed

  • Geothermal Generators sit on fixed geyser nodes and need no fuel — pure free power.
  • Output fluctuates with the geyser (roughly 100–400 MW depending on purity), so never make geothermal your base load.
  • Treat it as a free bonus layered on top of a stable coal/fuel/nuclear grid. Build it on every geyser you find; ignore the variability by over-provisioning your fueled generators.

Nuclear — the endgame

One building, a staggering output, and a waste problem you must solve before you flip it on.

Output2500 MW
Fuel Rods / min0.2
Water / min240 m³
Uranium Waste / min10
Solve waste first

Each plant spits out 10 Uranium Waste/min that never stops accumulating. Before building nuclear, have a plan: store it in an ever-growing container farm, or (better) process it through the Plutonium chain into Plutonium Fuel Rods. Nuclear Waste is radioactive — wear a Hazmat Suit and keep it away from walkways.

Nuclear is enormous power density: a single plant replaces dozens of coal generators. But it demands serious uranium logistics (mining, Encased Cells, fuel rod manufacturing, sulfuric acid) and water. Build it when you're ready to commit a region to it.

Grid rules that save your save

  • Always run a buffer above demand. Keep 10–20% headroom so spinning up a new factory block doesn't trip the grid.
  • Use Power Switches to segment. Put new/experimental factories behind a switch so a runaway draw can't black out your whole base. The 1.2 Priority Power Switch lets you shed non-critical load automatically when supply dips.
  • Storage = uptime insurance. Power Storage banks charge during surplus and discharge during spikes — a wall of them turns a brief deficit into a non-event.
  • Watch the Power Consumption multiplier. If you started a 1.2 save with raised Power Consumption, underclock machines (see clock math) instead of overclocking — the exponential cost curve will wreck you otherwise.
  • Daisy-chain to cut pole spam (1.2). The Upgraded Power Connectors MAM unlock lets a building take two power connections, so you can run power straight from building to building instead of dropping a pole for every machine. It's in the Caterium research tree — costs 15 Computers, 50 High-Speed Connectors, 500 Quickwire. Wire your main line into the chain, not a forest of poles.
