Final Fantasy VI

Pixel Remaster

A 14-character ensemble RPG split across two worlds. There are no jobs — each hero has a unique Command, and everyone can become a magic god through the Esper system. The depth is in stat min-maxing on level-up and a handful of broken relic combos. This guide is spoiler-light on story beats.

01 · The Shape

Overview & core systems

  • No job system. Each of the 14 characters has a fixed, unique Command (Sabin's Blitz, Edgar's Tools, Cyan's Bushido, etc.) on top of Fight / Magic / Item.
  • Magic is universal. Almost anyone can learn almost any spell by equipping Espers (Magicite) and earning Magic AP from battles. By the late game your whole roster can cast.
  • Two worlds. The World of Balance is the linear first half; the World of Ruin is an open post-apocalypse where you re-recruit the cast in (mostly) any order and can grind freely.
  • Relics are accessory slots (two each) that grant passive abilities, extra commands, or status immunity — the real build system sits here.
  • The winning idea: level up while the right Espers are equipped to pump a stat (usually Magic), then stack relic combos. A min-maxed party trivializes the endgame.
02 · Magicite

Espers & Magicite

Equip one Esper per character. Three things happen:

  • You learn its spells. Each Esper teaches a set list of spells at a learning rate (e.g. x10 = 10% of Magic AP per battle toward that spell). Win battles with the Esper equipped until the spell hits 100%.
  • You can Summon it once per battle for a powerful effect (and some summons also buff or revive).
  • You gain its level-up stat bonus (see Min-Maxing) — the single most important long-term mechanic.

03 · The Real Power

Stat min-maxing on level-up

This is the system that separates a casual run from a god party. While an Esper is equipped, every level-up grants that Esper's stat bonus (e.g. Magic +2, Strength +1, Speed +1, or HP/MP +X%).

  • Commit to one stat per character. For casters, level up only while wearing Magic +1/+2 Espers — a fully min-maxed Magic stat makes Ultima delete bosses.
  • Equip the bonus Esper at the lowest possible level. The earlier you start stacking the bonus, the more level-ups it applies to.
  • HP/MP +X% is a multiplier on that level's gain, not your max pool — so it also wants to be equipped early and consistently.
  • Most players over-level in the World of Ruin anyway, so plan which Esper each character wears before you grind there.
If you only remember one thing about FF VI optimization: level up with Magic-boosting Espers equipped. A maxed-Magic Terra, Celes, or Relm with dualcast Ultima is the endgame.

05 · Tier Talk

Best characters

  • Celes — the cleanest top pick: Runic shuts down magic bosses, and she's an excellent caster and fighter besides.
  • Terra — Morph + maxed Magic + dualcast = the highest magic ceiling, plus solid physicals.
  • Sabin — Blitz (Bum Rush) ignores defense formulas and never needs MP; carries the early/mid game single-handedly.
  • Edgar — Tools give consistent, targetable, MP-free damage with zero setup. The reliability pick.
  • Setzer — with Fixed Dice + Master's Scroll, a defense-ignoring physical machine.
  • Relm / Strago — Relm has the best Magic stat (monstrous once geared); Strago's Mighty Guard and Grand Train are genuinely top-tier support/damage.
  • Mog & Gogo — utility kings: Mog's Moogle Charm trivializes navigation, Gogo can copy your best build.
Honest truth: by the late game, any character with maxed Magic + dualcast + 1-MP spells is a god. Tiers matter most for the early/mid game, where Sabin and Edgar shine.

07 · Two Worlds

World of Balance → World of Ruin

The game pivots at the end of the World of Balance. In the World of Ruin you start alone as Celes and rebuild the party across an open map.

  1. Solitary Island: the WoR opens with Celes; the path forward is gated behind a small, famous sequence — persevere to reach the mainland.
  2. Get the airship (the Falcon, via Setzer in Kohlingen) to open the world. From here, recruitment is largely your choice.
  3. Re-recruit everyone: Edgar & Sabin, then optional hunts — Locke (Phoenix Cave, earns the Phoenix Esper), Terra (Mobliz), Cyan (Mt. Zozo), Strago & Relm (Thamasa / Ebot's Rock), Gau (the Veldt), Mog & Umaro (Narshe caves), Shadow (the Veldt, if you saved him), and Gogo (inside the Zone Eater).
  4. Grind with intent: before mass-leveling, equip each character's stat Esper. Then clear the Eight Dragons and Kefka's Tower.
Don't lose ShadowAt the climax of the World of Balance (the Floating Continent escape), wait for the full countdown before boarding the airship. Leave early and Shadow dies permanently — and you'll never get him (or his ultimate weapon) in the World of Ruin.
08 · Loadouts

Build archetypes

  1. Dualcast Ultima nuker — maxed-Magic caster (Terra / Celes / Relm) + Soul of Thamasa + Celestriad. Two 1-MP Ultimas every turn. Pre-cast Quick for a burst of four.
  2. 8-hit physical — Genji Glove + Master's Scroll + two top weapons. On Setzer/Locke, use Fixed Dice to ignore defense entirely.
  3. Sabin engine — Blitz Bum Rush needs no MP and ignores most defense; pair with Hero Ring and just win the early/mid game.
  4. Support fortress — Strago Mighty Guard (party Protect/Shell/Float/Haste-ish buffs), Golem/Phantom summons, Reflect rings to bounce magic.
  5. Runic wall — Celes holds Runic against magic-heavy bosses, eating their nukes and refueling MP while the rest of the party kills.
09 · The Hunt

Bosses, the Eight Dragons & the finale

  • The Eight Legendary Dragons (World of Ruin) are the optional superboss set — scattered across the map and Kefka's Tower, each with its own element/quirk. Defeating all eight unlocks the Crusader Esper.
  • Atma Weapon (Floating Continent) and Ultima Weapon (Kefka's Tower) are the marquee mid/late bosses — Atma scales damage to your HP, so a Reflect/heal plan matters.
  • Doom Gaze roams the WoR skies in the airship — chip it down across encounters; it's tied to the Bahamut Esper.
  • Kefka's Tower is a three-party gauntlet: split your roster into three balanced teams (each wants healing + damage), then the multi-tier final battle. Reflect, Ribbon, and a dualcast nuker close it out.
Colosseum (WoR): bet items to fight 1-on-1 for rare rewards (including Fixed Dice and other build pieces). Some of the best gear in the game is won here — look up exact wagers before betting anything irreplaceable.
10 · Don't Miss

Missables & secrets

  • Shadow — the #1 permanent miss (wait for the Floating Continent timer). If saved, find him sleeping at the Veldt inn area in the WoR — don't leave the Veldt before he appears.
  • Cursed Shield → Paladin's Shield — equip the Cursed Shield and survive ~255 battles to lift the curse, turning it into one of the best shields in the game (huge elemental absorb/null).
  • Ragnarok — in Narshe you choose between the Ragnarok Esper (teaches Ultima) and the Ragnarok sword. Most players take the Esper; the sword can also be won/obtained, so weigh it for your run.
  • Gogo & Umaro — both are easy to walk past: Gogo hides inside the Zone Eater on the Triangle Island; Umaro is in the Narshe cave (you need Mog along).
  • Steal early and often — Locke with the Thief's/Brigand's Glove can Capture rare drops from bosses you can't get otherwise.
  • The Veldt & Rages — Gau learns from monsters there; a few Rages (and the Stray Cat trick) carry whole stretches of the game.
11 · Field Notes

Durable tips

  • Plan your Esper-per-character before you grind. Magic for casters; Speed/Strength for dedicated physicals. Consistency beats raw levels.
  • Soul of Thamasa + Celestriad is the win button. Build at least one character around it.
  • Genji Glove + Master's Scroll for your physicals; Fixed Dice if you have it.
  • Keep a Runic Celes on standby for magic bosses, and a Mighty Guard Strago for party buffs.
  • Ribbons and Reflect answer the late-game status/magic spikes — especially in Kefka's Tower.
  • Use Mog's Moogle Charm when exploring long dungeons; it removes random encounters entirely.
  • Spread your party for Kefka's Tower — three viable teams, not one stacked team and two weak ones.
