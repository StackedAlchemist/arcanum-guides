The Roster

Character Builds

A company is not twelve copies of one soldier — each brother is a role. Every build below gives you the target stats, the talent stars to hunt for, the backgrounds to recruit, the weapons to carry, and a perk to take at every single level, 1 through 11.

How levelling actually works

A brother earns one perk point per level. Perks sit in seven tiers, and a tier only opens once you've bought enough perks in the tiers above it — so your early picks naturally climb the tree, and all seven tiers are reachable by about level 7. Your 11th and final perk comes at level 11; levelling past that does nothing in vanilla, so level 11 is the finished brother.

Because of this, a build's "perk order" is a level order. Each path below reads left to right: the number is the level, the name is the perk you buy at that level.

How to read a build — talents & stats

When recruiting, chase talent stars in a build's primary attributes: each star adds about +0.5 per level to that stat, i.e. roughly +5 by level 11 if you level it every time. A 1-star Poacher at 50 Ranged Skill ends up like a 0-star Hunter at 55. Starting values plus stars are what decide whether a recruit can reach the targets listed — not the background name alone. Always avoid the dealbreaker traits: Asthmatic, Dastard, and (on melee bros) Fainthearted/Irrational.

A Wallpike — Polearm Backline

Role: dependable second-row damage. Stabs over your front line from safety and punishes anyone engaging your back rank. Your answer to the Ancient Dead, where bows fail.

Melee Skill85+
Melee Def20+
Ranged Def15+ (or Anticipation)
Fatigue60-80
HP70-80

Talent stars: Melee Skill, Melee Defense, Ranged Defense. Backgrounds: cheap is fine — Farmhand, Militia, Thief, Brawler. The Billhook is the best weapon; the Pike adds hit chance vs dodgy foes.

1Colossus2Pathfinder3Backstabber4Polearm Mastery5Battle Forged6Anticipation7Berserk8Killing Frenzy9Brawny10Rotation11Quick Hands

Why & variants: Backstabber fixes the polearm's mediocre melee skill since you always attack engaged enemies. Polearm Mastery's reduced AP lets him step-and-stab in one turn. Take Quick Hands + Cleaver Mastery to carry a whip for disarming and armor-stripping — the best utility upgrade in the company.

A balanced company (12)

A clean default: 2-3 Anvils, 1 Banner Sergeant, 1 Wallpike, 3 ranged (mix Hawkeye / Bolt / Skirmisher), and 4-5 two-handers (Hammerfall + Reaper). Shift the ratio per enemy: more shields + bows vs Goblins; more two-handers and polearms (few shields) vs Orcs; shields-to-hold + two-handers vs the Ancient Dead, swapping bows for Wallpikes.
