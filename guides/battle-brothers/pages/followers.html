The Retinue

Company Followers

Followers don't swing a sword — they run the company behind it. Each is a non-combatant who hangs a permanent, company-wide bonus on your banner: cheaper recruits, faster healing, recovered ammo, fatter contracts. You get only a handful of slots, so the art is matching the follower to the stage of your campaign.

How the Retinue works

Followers are non-combatants you hire from the company screen (Blazing Deserts DLC) for a one-time crown cost — no daily wage. How many you can keep is gated by company renown: the 1st slot opens at Recognized (250 renown), 2nd at Competent (750), 3rd at Reputable (1,400), 4th at Great (2,250), and the 5th and final at Fabled (3,350). So early on you'll have just one — choose carefully.

A follower can be replaced at any time, so the roster isn't permanent — rotate as your needs change. A few have a prerequisite (the Lookout wants you to have discovered some locations yourself first). The same screen also upgrades your cart for inventory space.

How many can you have? Renown slots

Slots are unlocked purely by company renown — the tiers between these thresholds don't add a slot, so these are the breakpoints that matter. Five is the maximum.

Best picks by stage

You won't have many slots, so don't spread thin — take the two or three that solve your current problem.

Early game — you get ONE slot

Pick the single biggest leak

  • Surgeon — guarantees fragile bros survive a fatal blow. The safest first pick while losses end runs.
  • Scout — +15% travel speed = more contracts and crowns per day; never a bad buy.
  • Recruiter — if you still need bodies, cheaper hires + a bigger pool builds the roster.
Mid game — 2–3 slots

Snowball renown & income

  • Minstrel — +15% renown unlocks your next follower slots (and the noble houses) sooner.
  • Scout if you skipped it — still the best crowns-per-day buy.
  • Scavenger — ammo + tools start paying for themselves as fights pile up.
  • Cartographer — if you're still exploring, scouting becomes steady cash.
Late game — 4–5 slots

Sustain crises & a big roster

  • Paymaster — −15% wages + −50% desertion; the biggest saver on a veteran roster.
  • Quartermaster + Scavenger — keep ammo, tools and medicine flowing through long crisis fights.
  • Blacksmith — repair heavy plate between back-to-back battles (and recover dead men's gear).
  • Bounty Hunter — farm Champions for named gear and crowns once you can kill them easily.
Good at any time

Near-universal value

  • Scout — the most-picked follower; map speed helps every company.
  • Surgeon — saving even one veteran pays for it many times over.
  • Scavenger — ammo + tools matter in almost any roster.
  • Agent — map-wide contract visibility makes every travel decision smarter.
The one rule

A follower is worth its wage only if you're actually using the thing it improves. A Cartographer is gold while you're exploring and dead weight once you've mapped the region; a Scavenger is wasted on a company that never throws a javelin. Re-read your last ten days of play — where are you losing crowns, time, ammo or brothers? Hire the follower that plugs that leak, and swap it out when the leak moves.
