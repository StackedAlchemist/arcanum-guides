The Retinue

Company Followers

Followers don't swing a sword — they run the company behind it. Each is a non-combatant who hangs a permanent, company-wide bonus on your banner: cheaper recruits, faster healing, recovered ammo, fatter contracts. You get only a handful of slots, so the art is matching the follower to the stage of your campaign.

How the Retinue works

You hire followers from the company screen for an up-front cost plus a daily wage. Slots unlock as your company renown rises — the higher your renown tier (Competent, Professional…), the more followers you can keep and the better the ones on offer. Some have requirements before they'll join (the Lookout, for example, wants you to have discovered 10 locations yourself).

You can swap a follower for another at any time for a fee, so the roster isn't permanent — rotate them as your needs change. Treat the wage like any upkeep: a follower has to pay for itself in saved crowns, saved time, or saved brothers.

Best picks by stage

You won't have many slots, so don't spread thin — take the two or three that solve your current problem.

Early game

Build a roster & stop bleeding men

  • Recruiter — more, cheaper recruits to assemble a real company fast.
  • Drill Sergeant — +20% XP rushes green bros to usable levels.
  • Surgeon — turns would-be deaths into survivable injuries while you're fragile.
Mid game

Scale income, renown & logistics

  • Negotiator — faster relations/renown toward the noble-house unlock.
  • Scavenger — ammo + tools start paying for themselves as fights pile up.
  • Cartographer — if you're still exploring, scouting becomes steady cash.
  • Paymaster — once the roster grows, rein in the daily wage bill.
Late game

Sustain crises & a big roster

  • Paymaster — a full veteran roster's upkeep is brutal; this is the biggest saver.
  • Quartermaster + Scavenger — keep ammo and tools flowing through long crisis fights.
  • Blacksmith — repair heavy plate between back-to-back battles.
  • Surgeon — still earns its keep against lethal late enemies.
Good at any time

Near-universal value

  • Scavenger — ammo + tools matter in almost every company.
  • Lookout — vision to pick fights and avoid ambushes, all game.
  • Drill Sergeant — you're nearly always levelling someone.
The one rule

A follower is worth its wage only if you're actually using the thing it improves. A Cartographer is gold while you're exploring and dead weight once you've mapped the region; a Scavenger is wasted on a company that never throws a javelin. Re-read your last ten days of play — where are you losing crowns, time, ammo or brothers? Hire the follower that plugs that leak, and swap it out when the leak moves.
