Named & Legendary Items

The red glow and the blue glow. Named (Famed) items are ordinary gear that rolled randomized bonuses — better numbers, a former owner's name. Legendary items are fixed uniques with effects no smith can forge. Here's exactly what a named item can roll, and what every Legendary does.

How Named items roll

A named item starts from a base weapon/armor, then improves a few stats by random amounts inside fixed bands. Durability is always modified, then two other stats are improved. The tables below show the resulting possible min–max ranges — an actual drop lands somewhere inside them, so use the high end as the "god-roll" ceiling, not a guarantee.

Where they drop

Attackable locations can hold one or two named items beyond what champions carry. The chance scales with the location's resources and distance from the nearest settlement — far-flung Orc/Goblin Settlements, Barbarian Sanctuaries and Undead Necropolises/Buried Castles have the best odds (roughly +33% base before the distance bonus), while small hideouts are near-zero. Each item is about equally likely to be a weapon, shield, helmet or armor, and a location's own theme item is twice as likely as a generic one. Legendaries come from specific legendary locations and crisis rewards.
