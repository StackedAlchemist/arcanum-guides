Whispered in Taverns

Secrets & Edge Cases

The contract board won't tell you about the lunatic in the ice cave or the trick that turns an enemy giant against its handlers. Here is the knowledge that separates a careful company from a doomed one.

Hidden mechanics

The Beastmaster trick

Barbarian Beastmasters whip captive Unholds into fighting for them. Kill the Beastmaster and his Unholds turn hostile to the Barbarians. The flip side: if an Unhold ever ends up on your side (a "fight together" event), it can go wild and switch on you if its handler dies, sometimes as early as turn 2. Treat any Unhold's loyalty as conditional on the Beastmaster living.

Pre-loaded ranged

Crossbows and Handgonnes equipped in an active weapon slot at the start of battle are pre-loaded. Your crossbowmen and gunners get their first shot off immediately on turn one. Always start the fight with the ranged weapon in hand, not stowed.

Decapitation stops resurrection

Cutting-damage skills that behead (polearm Strike/Reap, 2H sword skills) permanently remove an undead corpse so a Necromancer can't raise it again. In a Necromancer fight, beheading the risen is how you actually make progress instead of fighting the same zombie six times.

Head hits and armor

A hit to the head does +50% damage to HP but not to armor. That's why flails (direct head targeting) and high-Melee-Skill daggers can kill a target whose armor you'd never break through normally. It's also why a helmet matters more than you'd think.

Disarm the dead

An unarmed Wiederganger can't hurt you. If you pick up a fallen enemy's weapon (even mid-fight), a risen version of that corpse has nothing to fight with. A cheap way to defang the Undead horde.

Legendary locations & named loot

The Cleaver Lunatic (ice caves, far North)

A crazed lone Barbarian lives in random ice caves in the North. Beating him requires a heavily armed, heavily armored brother who can win a one-on-one duel, ideally a 2H mace or hammer user with Battle Forged, strong Melee Attack and Defense. Win and you claim his special Head Chopper Orc cleaver and his broken armor (repairable into a strong piece).

Champions are loot piñatas

Champion enemies (marked with a star, unique gear, extra perks) always drop unique equipment when killed. By the late game you should be hunting champions specifically. They're harder than the rank and file, but the named weapon or armor they drop is often better than anything you can buy.

Legendary / named weapons

Weapons from bosses, special camps, and unusual circumstances roll randomized stats. That means a named weapon can be garbage, or it can be far better than any craftable equivalent. Note (v1.5.2.2 era balance): named weapons no longer roll reduced base Fatigue on equip, though reduced Fatigue build-up on skill use is still possible. Judge each named drop on its actual rolled stats, not the name.

Origins worth knowing

Battle Brothers has multiple campaign Origins that change the whole run. A few with notable secrets or playstyles:

  • Lone Wolf: start with one elite brother. Builds around the Lone Wolf perk and a single dominant fighter. High risk, high skill ceiling.
  • Gladiators: versatile ranged-and-melee fighters thrive here. Throwing weapons and adaptable kits pay off.
  • Peasant Militia: cheap, numerous, weak recruits. A "Roman legion" mass-spear-and-shield approach carries the early game.
  • Each origin shifts hiring pools and starting gear, so the "best" build list changes with your origin. Adapt the roster on the Builds page to what your origin can actually recruit.

The things that quietly kill runs

  • Permanent injuries. A broken elbow or collapsed lung permanently lowers attributes. Pull a hurt brother out with Rotation before he takes the second hit that maims him for good.
  • Fatigue exhaustion. A tired brother can't act. In long crisis fights, Recover and Mastery's Fatigue cut are what keep your damage online.
  • Night fights vs ranged enemies. Reduced vision favors the side that doesn't need to see far. Avoid fighting Goblins and Orcs at night if you can.
  • Greed. The four-skull contract that's "probably fine" is how veterans die. If you're not sure you can win, you can't afford to find out.
Last word

Retreat is a valid strategy. The map respawns enemies and contracts; it does not respawn the brother you raised from a Farmhand into a Battle-Forged monster over sixty in-game days. When in doubt, buy this game's most underrated item: time.
