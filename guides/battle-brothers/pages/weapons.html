Weapons & Skills

There is no single best weapon, only the right tool for the enemy in front of you. A hammer opens a knight. A cleaver bleeds a giant. A dagger ignores armor entirely. Know what each family does before you spend a perk on its Mastery.

Reading the numbers

Damage is the roll vs. HP. Against armor it's scaled by Vs. Armor %, so a 40×130% axe shreds plate while a 40×60% dagger barely dents it — but the dagger's Puncture ignores armor entirely. A head hit adds ×1.5 to HP (not armor), which is why high-Head two-handers and the Killer/Executioner perks hit so hard. Orc weapons (Tree Limb, Cudgel, Head Splitter) cost +5 fatigue per skill for human wielders — note their brutal Max Fat. already.

Range & accuracy rules

Range is measured in tiles. Bows reach 7 tiles (most of them); crossbows and slings reach 6; thrown weapons reach 4; the Handgonne is a 2-tile weapon that punches through cover. Shooting downhill adds +1 range per height level above the target (uphill loses it). Accuracy drops with distance per the firing skill below, and a head hit deals ×1.5 to HP (not to armor), so high-range, high-accuracy shooters reward the head-hit perks.

Damage rules to remember

A head hit deals 50% bonus damage to HP but not to armor. Orc weapons cost 5 extra Fatigue per skill for human wielders, so put them on high-Fatigue brothers like Wildmen or Barbarians. Each tier of a weapon is a straight upgrade, so there is no reason to keep a lower-tier version once you find a better one.
