11 · Growth

The Peninsula of Power & leveling

  • The Peninsula of Power — a thin strip of land east of Pravoka where unusually strong, high-EXP enemies appear early. Fight there (carefully) to over-level your party fast. It still exists in the PR.
  • Pixel Remaster boosts make grinding optional. The settings menu has EXP and Gil multipliers and an encounter toggle — turn EXP up if you want to breeze through, or off to explore without random battles.
  • Autobattle repeats your last commands — great for clearing trash quickly without menu fatigue.
  • Level for the Fiends, not the dungeons. If a Fiend is hitting too hard, a few levels and Protect/Haste usually solve it faster than better gear.
12 · This Version

What the Pixel Remaster changed

  • Intelligence works — black magic finally scales with the caster's Int, so dedicated mages outgrow Red Mage late.
  • Buff & resist spells fixed — Temper, Saber and the NulX resist line all do what they say now.
  • Item-cast spells work — weapons/items that cast spells when used in battle (like the Masamune's Haste) function correctly.
  • Smoother balance & QoL — rebalanced magic, modern UI, bestiary, world map, auto-battle, and save anywhere.
  • No bonus dungeons. The Soul of Chaos dungeons and other GBA/PSP extras are not included — the PR is purely the rebalanced original.
If you own the PRThis guide matches the Pixel Remaster you have on Steam/console/mobile. Spell and monster names use the PR localization (Curaga, Marilith, etc.), and everything above — the charge system, the three-of-four spell limit, fixed Int, no bonus dungeons — is specific to this version.
13 · Field Notes

Durable tips

  • Plan your spell trios. You can only learn three of four spells per level — decide before you buy. Skip the weak fourth (often a redundant or niche spell).
  • Buy Haste as soon as it's available. It's the highest-impact spell for a physical party, period.
  • Carry Ethers for boss runs — charges don't refill mid-dungeon without them or an inn.
  • Spread Ribbons and resist gear to the party members most likely to get one-shot by status (your casters).
  • Re-equip after Class Change — Knights and Ninjas can suddenly use much better gear you may already be carrying.
  • A Monk needs no weapon. Don't waste gil arming your Black Belt/Master — bare fists are the point.
