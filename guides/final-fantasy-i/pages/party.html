The Four

Classes & Party Building

Your one big decision is made on the character-creation screen: which four of the six classes carry the entire game. There are no recruits to fix a weak pick — so understand what each class becomes before you lock it in.

01 · The Six

The starting classes

Each class has a clear role and a clear weakness. The three "fighters" carry the early game; the three mages are fragile but define your late game once Intelligence and the spell fixes kick in.

ClassRoleStrengthsWatch out
WarriorFrontline tank / DPSBest HP, armor and weapons; reliable damage all game.No magic of its own (until Knight).
ThiefSpeed / utilityHigh evasion & luck, boosts the party's run-away chance; blossoms after class change.Weakest hitter before becoming a Ninja.
Monk (Black Belt)Unarmed brawlerDamage scales with level — bare fists out-hit swords late; costs almost nothing in gear.Low defense; weak/awkward early.
Red MageHybrid jack-of-allDecent weapons/armor and both white & black magic; the easy-mode generalist.Caps below pure casters; can't learn level-8 spells.
White MageHealer / supportThe full white list to level 8 — heals, revives, buffs, resists, and Holy.Frail; poor physical damage.
Black MageNukerThe full black list to level 8; with fixed Intelligence it scales the hardest of anyone.Lowest HP/defense in the game.
PR change — why mages are good nowIn the original NES game the Intelligence stat did nothing, so mages fell off and Red Mage out-scaled the dedicated casters. The Pixel Remaster fixed Intelligence, so Black Wizard and White Wizard now pull clearly ahead of Red Wizard in the late game.
02 · The Upgrade

The Class Change

About two-thirds through the story you complete the Citadel of Trials to earn the Rat's Tail, then take it to Bahamut, King of Dragons, in the Cavern of Dragons. He promotes all four Light Warriors at once into their advanced forms. This is a permanent upgrade, not a switch — your class identity stays, it just gets stronger and gains new options.

BecomesWhat it gains
Warrior → KnightThe best gear in the game and low-level white magic (levels 1–3). Can wield Excalibur.
Thief → NinjaBig jump in weapons/armor and low-level black magic (levels 1–4). The standout of class change — a fighter that also casts.
Monk → MasterEven higher unarmed damage and multi-hits — arguably the top raw physical DPS late.
Red Mage → Red WizardMagic up to level 7 in both schools, still solid weapons/armor. Versatile, never specialized.
White Mage → White WizardThe complete white list, including Holy and full-party heals/revives.
Black Mage → Black WizardThe complete black list — Flare, Quake, the -aga line, Warp, Stop, Death.
Do the Rat's Tail. Class Change is the single biggest power spike in the game. Don't skip the Citadel of Trials — an un-promoted party hits a wall against the back-half bosses.
03 · The Lineup

Party compositions

Any four classes can finish the game in the PR, but some lineups are far smoother. A good party wants a tank, a damage core, healing, and offensive magic.

PartyWhy it worksFor
Warrior · Thief · White Mage · Black MageThe classic. Tank, speedy second fighter, full healing, full nuking. Thief becomes a fantastic Ninja.First-timers wanting balance
Warrior · Red Mage · White Mage · Black MageMagic-leaning powerhouse: two strong casters, a flexible Red, and a tank. Excellent in the PR now that Int works.Players who like spells
Warrior · Monk · White Mage · Black MageTwo heavy hitters up front (Master is a wrecking ball late) with full magic support.Strong, slightly squishier
Warrior · Warrior · Red Mage · White MageBeginner-friendly wall — tanky and forgiving, with healing and a flex caster. Low magic ceiling.Easiest, lowest risk
4× Monk / 4× Red MageMeme/challenge runs. Four Masters is brutally strong late but a slog early; four Red Wizards is a do-everything novelty.Replays / challenge
Rule of thumb: always bring one White Mage line for healing and one damage frontliner (Warrior or Monk). Everything else is preference. Avoid an all-mage party on a first run — the early game with no real frontline is rough.
