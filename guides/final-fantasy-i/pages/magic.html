The Spells

The Magic System

FF I's magic is the oldest, strangest version in the series — no shared MP pool, a limited number of casts per spell level, and a hard limit on which spells each caster can ever learn. The Pixel Remaster keeps the structure but fixes the bugs underneath it.

04 · Charges

Spell levels & charges, not MP

  • Eight spell levels. Magic is organized into levels 1–8, each with four spells per school (white and black). Higher levels are stronger and unlock as you progress.
  • Charges are pooled by level, not by spell. You don't have MP — you have a number of charges for each spell level. Casting any level-3 spell spends one level-3 charge, whatever the specific spell.
  • Charges grow as you level up (and the count benefits from the caster's stats). Inns restore them; Ethers restore charges in the field.
  • You learn spells from shops. White and black magic shops sell spell tomes by level. Buying teaches that spell to a compatible class — permanently.
The big restrictionEach caster can only ever learn three of the four spells at every spell level — once a character knows three at a given level, the fourth slot is closed. Plan the trio per level instead of buying blindly.
05 · White

White magic — the must-buys

White magic is healing, revival, buffs, and protection. Your White Mage line should prioritize sustain and the resist/haste tools that win the long boss fights.

06 · Black

Black magic — the must-buys

Black magic is raw damage plus a few priceless utilities. With Intelligence working in the PR, your Black Wizard's nukes scale into the late game instead of fizzling.

Fire / Blizzard / Thunder → -ra → -agaThe elemental damage core — match the Fiend weaknesses (Fire→Lich, Blizzard→Marilith, Thunder→Kraken).Always

Buy Haste early. A Hasted Knight, Master or Ninja roughly doubles its physical output — it's the strongest single thing your Black Mage line does for the team in boss fights.
PR change — bugs fixedThe NES original had a graveyard of broken spells: resist spells (NulX) gave no protection, Temper/Saber did nothing, and Intelligence didn't boost damage. The Pixel Remaster repairs all of this — buffs, resists and item-cast spells now behave as their descriptions say.
