01 · The Shape

Overview & the job system

  • Jobs from Crystals. Each shattered Crystal grants a batch of Jobs. A character set to a Job earns ABP in battle, raising that Job's level and permanently teaching its abilities.
  • Mix and match. Beyond the Job's innate command, you equip one secondary ability (a learned command or passive) from any other job. This cross-class slot is the entire build system.
  • Master a Job (max its level) to unlock its best ability and, crucially, carry its innate passives onto the Freelancer and Mime jobs.
  • Freelancer & Mime are the endgame vessels. Freelancer inherits the innate abilities and best stats of every mastered Job (2 ability slots); Mime has no innate command but equips three abilities and inherits passives. Stack your mastered combos here.
  • The winning idea: master a handful of key jobs, then run Freelancer/Mime with Dual-Wield + Rapid Fire (physical) or Dualcast (magic).
Pixel Remaster noteThe PR is based on the SNES original: 22 jobs, and the optional superbosses Omega and Shinryu in the Rift. It does not include the GBA Advance extras (Gladiator, Cannoneer, Oracle, Necromancer jobs or the Sealed Temple / Enuo). It does add the usual PR quality-of-life (bestiary, boosts, updated translation: Bartz, Lenna, Faris, Galuf, Krile, Exdeath, Gilgamesh).

03 · The Engine

The secondary-ability engine

  • Learn it once, keep it forever. ABP unlocks a Job's abilities permanently; switch Jobs freely and slot a learned ability as your secondary.
  • Passives stack onto Freelancer/Mime. Master Ninja and Freelancer keeps Dual-Wield innately; master Monk and it keeps Barehanded/Counter; etc. This is how you assemble a god character.
  • Mime is the combo chassis — three ability slots let you run, say, !Dualcast + !Summon + !Time on one character, all inheriting mastered passives.
  • Don't sleep on cheap masteries: Monk early (HP), Black/White/Time for your casters, then the four pillars below.
04 · The Exploits

Broken combos

  1. Dual-Wield + Rapid Fire — equip two strong weapons (Ninja), then !Rapid Fire (Ranger) for 8 hits that ignore defense and evasion. The premier physical build.
  2. Spellblade + Rapid Fire + Dual-Wield — enchant your weapons with !Spellblade (Flare, Holy, or instant-Death), then Rapid Fire to spray enchanted hits or one-shot whole rooms.
  3. Dualcast nuker — master Red Mage for !Dualcast, slot offensive Black/Summon/Time magic, and fire two Flares / Meteors / Bahamuts per turn. Pre-cast Quick (Time) for four.
  4. !Mix (Chemist) — Goliath Tonic doubles a character's max HP; Dragon Power buffs stats; other mixes nuke or full-revive. Cheap ingredients, huge payoffs.
  5. !Zeninage (Samurai) — throw gil for guaranteed big nonelemental AoE; trivializes crowds if you're rich.
The two pillars: physical carries want Dual-Wield + Rapid Fire (add Spellblade for elements/instakill); magic carries want Dualcast. Master those four jobs (Ninja, Ranger, Mystic Knight, Red Mage) and the back half of the game folds.
05 · Enemy Skills

Blue Magic worth grabbing

Equip Learning (Blue Mage) and survive an enemy casting the spell — or use Beastmaster !Control to force it. Standouts:

  • Mighty Guard — party-wide Protect + Shell (+ Float). One of the best buffs in the game.
  • White Wind — heals the whole party for the caster's current HP. Free, scaling, no MP-heavy cost.
  • Level 5 Death — instantly kills every enemy whose level is a multiple of 5 (works on some bosses — check levels).
  • Aero / Aera / Aeroga — reliable early wind damage before you have Black magic.
  • Goblin Punch, Death Claw, Time Slip, Aqua Breath, Missile — situational gems for control and damage.
06 · Priorities

Best jobs to master

  • Ninja — Dual-Wield is non-negotiable for any physical build.
  • Ranger — Rapid Fire is the best physical command in the game.
  • Mystic Knight — Spellblade turns those hits into elemental/instant-death.
  • Red Mage — Dualcast doubles every nuke; the magic equivalent of Rapid Fire.
  • Monk (early HP), Black / Time / Summon (your caster's spell lists), Chemist (Mix utility), Samurai (Zeninage + Shirahadori).
  • Then settle into Freelancer or Mime with the abilities above stacked.
07 · The Arsenal

Legendary weapons & the Knife dilemma

The third world hides the twelve legendary weapons (Excalibur, Ragnarok, Masamune, Apollo's Harp, Holy Lance, Sage's Staff, Assassin's Dagger, Yoichi's Bow, and more) behind the sealed tablets. A few are build-defining: Masamune grants innate Haste and first-strike; Apollo's Harp deals 4× damage to Exdeath; Assassin's Dagger can instant-kill.

Chicken Knife vs Brave Blade. You can only take one. The Brave Blade starts strong but loses power every time you flee a battle; the Chicken Knife grows stronger the more battles you've fled (but can randomly make a character flee). For a Dual-Wield + Rapid Fire run, the Chicken Knife is usually the pick — its ceiling is enormous and a stray flee barely matters.
08 · The Journey

The three worlds

  • First World — Bartz's world; you earn the Wind/Water/Fire/Earth Crystal job batches as the story breaks each Crystal.
  • Second World — Galuf's world and Exdeath's seat of power; the cast and the threat escalate, more jobs and gear open up.
  • Third World — the two worlds merge. This is the open endgame: the sealed legendary weapons, optional bosses, the Phantom Village, and the Interdimensional Rift (Cleft of Dimensions), the final dungeon that also houses the superbosses.
09 · The Hunt

Bosses & superbosses

  • Gilgamesh — the series' breakout recurring boss debuts here (and the "Clash on the Big Bridge" theme). He shows up several times; later he's almost an ally.
  • Exdeath → Neo Exdeath — the final fight is a multi-part gauntlet; Apollo's Harp, Mighty Guard, and a Dualcast/Rapid-Fire carry close it.
  • Omega (Rift) — a mechanical superboss immune to most things and very fast. Rapid Fire + the right enchant, Reflect/Shell, and careful turns; drops the Omega Badge.
  • Shinryu (Rift) — opens by casting Tidal Wave, so enter with water immunity (Coral Ring) or it's an instant wipe. Ribbons for its status, then burst it down for the Dragon Seal / Ragnarok payoff.
  • Gil Turtle, Movers, and other optional fights reward rare gear and big ABP.
10 · Don't Miss

Missables & secrets

  • Blue Magic windows — a few enemy skills are easy to skip; most are re-learnable on Rift enemies, but grab Mighty Guard, White Wind, and Level 5 Death when you can (use Control to force them).
  • The twelve legendary weapons — don't leave the third world without sweeping the sealed tablets / Phantom Village shops.
  • Chicken Knife / Brave Blade — a permanent either/or; pick deliberately (see Weapons).
  • !Catch rare monsters (Beastmaster) for one-use nukes you can't get otherwise.
  • Master before the merge where you can — ABP-rich spots (and the Rift's Movers) make finishing your key jobs painless before the finale.
11 · Field Notes

Durable tips

  • Master four jobs above all: Ninja (Dual-Wield), Ranger (Rapid Fire), Mystic Knight (Spellblade), Red Mage (Dualcast).
  • Build on Freelancer or Mime — that's where mastered passives and multi-slot combos come together.
  • Keep a Mighty Guard / White Wind Blue Mage for party buffs and free healing.
  • Goliath Tonic (Mix) to double a carry's HP before a hard boss.
  • Enter Shinryu with water immunity and everyone Ribbon'd; enter Omega expecting to lose a few attempts.
  • Apollo's Harp trivializes the Exdeath fights — bring a Bard or just the weapon.
  • Don't over-think the Knife choice — Chicken Knife + Rapid Fire is the path of least resistance.
