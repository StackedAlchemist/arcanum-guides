The Engine

The Growth System

FF II has no levels. Every character is shaped entirely by what they do in battle — which makes it the most freeform party-building in the classic era, and (in the Pixel Remaster) finally a smooth one.

01 · How Stats Grow

Do the thing, get better at the thing

After a battle, each stat has a chance to rise based on the actions you took with it. Use it, and it grows.

StatHow it grows
HPTake damage during the fight. (In the PR, total HP lost over the battle counts — you can heal back to full and still gain.)
MPSpend MP during the fight (same "total spent" rule in the PR).
StrengthAttack with physical weapons. Drives physical damage.
IntelligenceCast Black magic. Powers offensive spells.
SpiritCast White magic. Powers healing/support spells.
StaminaEndure hits; raises HP gains and resilience.
AgilityStay light (low equipment weight) and evade; drives evasion and turn speed.
02 · Proficiency

Weapon & spell levels

  • Weapons have skill levels per type (sword, axe, bow, knife, staff, etc.). The more you attack with a type, the higher its level — more accuracy and damage. Pick one weapon type per character and stick with it so it levels fast.
  • Spells have their own levels. Each spell (Fire, Cure, Berserk…) climbs with use, hitting harder/lasting longer and eventually costing less. Leveling spells is the slow part — commit to a small, focused spell set rather than dabbling.
  • Shields & Evasion level too — carrying a shield and getting attacked raises your evasion skill, so dedicated defenders earn their dodge.
Pixel Remaster noteThe PR removed the original's infamous friction: growth is consistent through normal play (no self-attacking to farm HP), HP/MP gains track total lost/spent in a battle, and the per-battle proficiency cap is gone — so skills and spells level much faster. You can just play and your party grows sensibly.
03 · The Principle

Specialize, don't spread

Because growth follows actions, the winning approach is focus. A character who attacks with one weapon type and casts two or three chosen spells becomes a powerhouse; one who dabbles in everything stays mediocre at all of it. Decide each character's lane early (see Characters & Builds) and feed it every battle.

A simple, reliable spread: one or two pure physical attackers (single weapon type), one Black caster (a few offensive spells), and a White caster (Cure + buffs) — with everyone learning Cure as a safety net since MP is freeform.
